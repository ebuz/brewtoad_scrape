Session Belgian Blonde

an All Grain Belgian Blond Ale by Phillip McDaniel

Brewed by Phillip McDaniel

On December 16, 2015

Brewing Notes

Dec 16, 2015 Wednesday - Brew Day for Session Belgian

Water treatment amounts in recipe. Very clear mash, decent conversion 4-5 pts below usual % attributed to lower diastatic power of mash. In future mash 90 minutes rather than 60 for this type of brew or up grist to compensate.

2.75 gallons mash water

Mash for 14 minutes @ 151.5 Mash for 35 minutes @ 157.5 Mash for 11 minutes @ 166.0 Mast for 15 minutes @ 170.0

2 gallons sparge water @ 170.0 Vourlaufed 3.5 gallons and runnings remained 30% cloudy. Cloudiest runnings ever for such a thorough vourlauf.
With vourlauf time of 14 minutes entire sparge took 33 minutes.
Collected 3.6 gallons of wort.
Topped boil pot with water to 6.6 gallons boil volume.

Took 35 minutes to come to a boil. Add wort chiller, hop spider, 4 teaspoons yeast nutrient, 1.5 teaspoons yeast energizer, & 1 whirlfloc tablet at 10 minutes. Add 1 oz hops @ 6 minutes. 15.3 aau for 12 ibu.

Very poor hot and cold breaks, virtually non-existent. (will need to fine with gelatin.

Chilled to 67, pitched yeast. Yeast was in flask for 3+ weeks covered by aluminum foil. Sample was very sour, like lacto sour, clean otherwise. Think it was the sample as the yeast starter was 5 days before showing activity. Thus the wort soured a bit prior to the yeast taking off. There was zero pelical.

Overall an easy brew day. Will keep it below 70 until active fermentation starts then let it do what it will.

Yeast pitched at 4:30 pm

Notes

Date Note
12/17/2015 6:10am

No signs of activity. Fermenter temp at 66 - 67.
12/19/2015 12:40pm

Activity has started. Fermenter temp has been in the 65 F range the entire time. Estimate active fermentation began at approximately 60-65 hours.

12/20/2015 12:43pm

Starting yesterday at about 1:00 pm (24 hours ago) I began using a heating pad under the bottom of the fermenter. Initially at low setting till it cycled off. Eventually last night I used the medium, used high this morning. Across 24 hours I've raised the fermenter temp from 64/65 to 68/69. Will continue raising temp into mid/high 70s. Activity has picked up tremendously as it was beginning to slow prior to application of heat.

12/21/2015 7:32am

Fermentation finished late today, early 12/22. Temp was at 69/70 at end of fermentation.

12/24/2015 7:32am

Fermentation complete, sitting at room temp.

01/01/2016 2:51am

Took hydrometer reading and it is at 1.012. Much higher than predicted by recipator but not unexpected. I was hoping for such a result, YES! This means I have an abv of 2.3%. Also have 'yeast brains' floating in the fermenter for the first time. COOL! Will transfer to secondary or keg with 1/4-1/2 pack gelatin for clarity.

Tasting notes: Nose is typical Belgian Abbey at first. Corriander and slightly spicy nose(there are no spices in the beer). Amber/Victory grain character comes through nicely along with grainy malt presence. Fermentation tastes clean/bug free which was a concern due to fermenter used. Finishes very tart, wonder what the pH is? Dry finish, well bittered for size of beer, drinkable, begs to have you take another sip. Carbonation is going to add a lot. No green apple flavor/aroma, yeast has finished cleaning up after itself. Has a good bit of belgian wit character.

On the downside it is not terribly complex and lacks a little bit in the esters department. Carbonation should again help bring out more esters. Btw this is not unexpected given the low fermentation temps.

01/02/2016 7:30pm

Transferred wort to keg. Was off-clear, disappointing actually. There was not a gelatin-sludge at the bottom either though there were clumps that appeared to be more yeast floc than gelatin. Set the CO2 to 35 & the fridge is at 32F. Checked carbonation, it's still weak, needs another 20-30 hrs. Slightly carbonic and bitterness is accentuated, clove and pepper spice dominate.

01/25/2016 2:02am

Final tasting notes:
Mouthfeel could not be more spot on for such a small beer. Head retention is nice but not spectacular, 3.5 stars out of 5, decent lacing. Clarity is brilliant. Aroma is muted but hints of Belgian spice come through along with subtle malt hints, hop character is also apparent and there is lots of overlapping between hop/phenol/ester characteristics. Flavor has nice but not assertive Belgian phenolics but muted esters, meaning nice spice but not enough fruity esters. The beer is clearly a bit more hoppy than the style should be but this is entirely a concept/non-styled beer. That said, the 1 oz of Calypso hops are fairly assertive in bitterness, just a bit too much, but they complement the beer flavors nicely with it's own spice and subtle apple/pear/very mild citrus character. Very drinkable, carbed to approximately 3 volumes, very dry, leaves you wanting another sip with each swallow.

Overall on a bjcp scale and knowing what I'm going for with a 2.3% brew this is a high 30 pt attempt, 37-38 pts. It might even hit low 40s with some judges while those expecting simply a reduced version of a Belgian Blonde would be disappointed. What is for sure is there could be more yeast character and warmer weather fermentation should give me that character. Final note, reduce bitterness & increase yeast character for perfection.
