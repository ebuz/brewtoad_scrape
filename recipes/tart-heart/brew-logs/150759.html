Tart Heart

an All Grain Berliner Weisse by h0psnobery

Export BeerXML

Brewed by h0psnobery

On August 18, 2017

Brewing Notes

Used http://www.biabcalculator.com to calculate strike temp. Assumed grain temp as 70F. Brought 3.5 gallons of strike water to 158F. Targeted Mash temp was 153F, hit 156F. I need to find a different calculator, this one seems to be consistently high. The mash temp dropped to 140F over the course of an hour. I need to either start mashing in an insulated cooler or applying heat during the mash. Boiled for 15 minutes with the lid on to sanitize the entire vessel. I left it covered in the garage over night to cool. Vessel was still warm in the morning. Pitched approximately 24 fl oz of a Mango Good Belly drink to inoculate wort with lactobacillus plantarum. Covered vessel with several layers of saran wrap to prevent oxygen ingress. Placed vessel in a large cube cooler with hot water to maintain warm wort temperature. Boiled again on 8/26/17 for 15 minutes with the lid on to sanitize the entire vessel and kill off lacto. Added 0.5 oz Centennial hops (8.4 AA) approximately 15 minutes before wort reached a boil. Cooled in the vessel in a cooler of ice water for a few hours. Pitched one packet of hydrated US-05 and aerated wort at midnight. Forgot to take OG reading. Activity noted at 8:00 am the following morning (8/27/17). Activity noted at 10 pm on Monday (8/28/17) and none at 6 am on Tuesday (8/29/17). I can think of two possibilities for the short fermentation period. The yeast was able to fully ferment the wort because of the low OG, or the yeast stalled because of the low PH from the lacto.

Added 1.5 oz Centennial hops (8.4 AA) on Saturday (9/2/17). Bottled one week later (9/9/17). FG measured 1.010, so the yeast appears to have worked despite the low PH.

Appearance: hazy straw yellow. Smell: sharp and citrusy with dusty sweetness like sweet tarts. Taste: lacks any destinct flavor. It's tart, but not quite sour. I was hoping for some citrus from the Centennial and mango from the Good Belly drink, but I cant find any. It's not bad, just not remarkable. Body: at just 3% abv I didn't expect any body at all, but I think the Crystal malt has helped keep it from being thin.

This is definitely a recipe to build on. Not only, can I make an IPA and a sour with the same malt/hops, but its also very little additional work to get a sour out of it. I think I may try to add some fruit in secondary to round out the flavor though.
