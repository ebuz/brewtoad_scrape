Fermentation Checks

Date Temperature Gravity Notes
01/27/2015 6:12am 24.0 degC 1.048

[1 day] Happy with 33% AA in the first 24 hours. Tasting incredible - really citrussy and juicy, with very subdued bitterness. Nicely tart in flavour too.
01/29/2015 9:16am 24.0 degC 1.022

[3 days] Have been using heat belt to keep this at 24-26°C most of the time. Accidentally left on and it got to 28-29°C at 48 hours, but should be okay with this yeast (max temp 29.5°C). Slowed down a fair bit now, bit very cloudy. Incredible flavour - some alcohol bite, but little bitterness and very juicy.
01/31/2015 10:44am 28.0 degC 1.017

[5 days] Used heat belt quite a bit. Dying out, but flavour is still nicely juicy. Definitely some bitter alcohol flavour, but hopefully this will mellow out. I'll switch heat belt off now and let this go for another week before racking and dry hopping in the keg.
02/03/2015 8:45pm 19.0 degC 1.014

[8.5 days] Tasting very juicy, with slightly less of the bitter alcohols from last sample. Almost ready for racking and dry hopping, but I'll give it another few days.
02/09/2015 4:47am 21.0 degC 1.013

[14 days] Not a lot more activity. Tasting really good - still a hint of alcohol, but acceptable for 8% abv. It's so hoppy I'm going to reduce the dry hop addition to 2g/L (and definitely do it in a purged keg so I don't lose aroma). I'd also like to Brett a few litres prior to dry hopping to see what it does.
02/19/2015 11:12pm 21.0 degC 1.009

[25 days] Racked into a CO2 flushed keg and added 50g bagged dry hops, then triple purged with CO2 after filling. Leaving at room temperature for 3 days, then removing hops and force carbing.
01/26/2015 7:30am 24.0 degC 1.072

[0 days] Racked off cold break after 4 hours, with lots of splashing to aerate. Also ran aquarium pump for 2 hours. Pitched about 40% of yeast cake from Sunshine (est. 450 billion cells, which is ideal). Warm pitch, and ambient should keep it at 24°C or above. Initial pH was 5.07.
{+12 hours] Very active, blowing out the airlock this morning. Sitting at 23°C. Smells awesome.
