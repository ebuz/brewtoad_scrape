Brewed by jll23

Brewing Notes

Mashing with a little under 1.75 gallons. About ~.5 of that is distilled and the rest is tap water treated for chlorine products with campden.

Will sparge with about 1.75 gallons of campden-treated tap water.

Cooled it down to about 82F and pitched as much of the yeast as I could get from the pack. Loosely wrapped it in a blanket and put it in a closet. I like not having to worry about this fermentation temperature much.

I first worted about half of the initial bittering charge.

During the cooling of the wort, I tossed in ~3 additional pellets for hopefully even more spicy hop goodness.

Taste

OG sample has a fair amount of bitterness to it. Decently light golden color. Not a lot of malt at this point, but it's also being hidden by the bitterness.

At bottling, this appears to be a pretty good facsimile for Dortmunder Gold. First off, it is a nice golden color (that's usually the aspect that is always a wild card for me). The hop aroma is really close. Even the slightly bready malt flavors seem pretty appropriate. Without carbonation, I'm not sure if the bitterness could be slightly more pronounced, but all in all this was a pretty good shot. As expected, there is a bit more fruit sweetness from the esters, as well as a little honey too. They do make the beer seem a bit sweeter than maybe it is, but they actually go quite well with everything else that's going on. Also, it should be noted that I got a pretty good FG this time. The first couple times I used this yeast, it ended kinda high. I seem to have got that straightened out through dialing in the mash temp. Seems like this could be a really good beer.

I've tried a few before this, but the one I'm currently having is about two weeks in the bottle. Beer already has the right level of carbonation, which scares me slightly. Then again, it has been a how two weeks for bottle conditioning. Right away I will say that the hop spice has really died down. There's still noble-ish hop flavor, but it's not as delightfully pungent as it was before. The malt flavors have really melded already too. It tastes like a slightly malty, slightly hoppy easy drinking beer. Has those subtle fruit and honey notes from the yeast. Still a good beer, just not as flavorful as it was right after bottling. It's hard to get the flavors to peak at the right times with some of these. I haven't actually dry hopped in forever, but I do wonder if that would have given me more of the hop flavor I desire. At the same time, much more hops in this and it would have risked becoming hop-tea.

Not long after the last one. A little over two weeks in bottle. The hops have faded some, but right now this is a really nice creamy, slightly malty, slightly fruity ale. The flavors are really melding well and what they create together is mild and lovely. This is the kind of vaguely European ale I like to make. It's got solid contributions from all the ingredients, yet nothing is terribly overshadowing the rest. There's room in that scenario for the various aspects to really come together into something subtle and unexpected. So far, in that regard this one seems really successful. Maybe someday I can find a way to turn up the unique herbal spice of these hops, but I'm hardly disappointed after this first attempt.
