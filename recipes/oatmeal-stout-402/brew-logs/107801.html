Oatmeal Stout

an All Grain Oatmeal Stout by ironhutales

Brewed by ironhutales

On December 02, 2013

Brewing Notes

Based loosely off of McQuaker's Oatmeal Stout from BCS. Cooked oats in one pot and spelt & flaked barley in another, then added to mash which bumped up temp to 72 - 73*C. Left lid off mash until it dropped to 70C, then covered for one hour. The temp held steady with blanket and sleeping bag for insulation.

Added the roasted grains to top of mash for sparge. sparge water was 78*C but maybe it should be lower if i get too much astringency from grain tannins. i should maybe monitor sparge and mash Ph if astringency is a problem in finished beer.

Grain bill seems complicated because I don't have crystal 80.. so I did a combo of German dark caramel and a tad Special B to get it ballpark. I also increased the % of these crystals and added a generous portion of German light crystal and the last of my carapils -- with the intention of adding body and f.g.

I didn't have an English yeast ready so I had to use Safale 05 which has been attenuating extremely high in my beers, hence the overall bump up in crystals and mash temp to compensate for the highly attentive American yeast. I really want some body in this one so it will be interesting to see what the f.g. after 05 chomps away the fermentables.

I had 29g of organic cocoa powder (10 - 12% fat/oil) which I thought could add a little bitter chocolate complexity and help balance this potentially cloyingly sweet beer. I decided to add cocoa at 15min after reading online and seeing the clonerecipe for Stone anniversary bitter Chocolate Oatmeal Stout in BYO (although that recipe is about 3x more cocoa than I used here).

I went for 45 ibu (instead of the 36 in BCS) because I began to think this beer could come out cloying with the high mash temp, high % of crystals, etc. despite the aggressive American yeast. I used the Styrian Goldings to give a little subtle background hop flavor as opposed to only the super clean Magnum @ 60min.

Dinkel (spelt) and flaked barley were added because I only had 400g oatmeal on-hand, and the recipe calls for 450g. We had lots of dinkel and flaked barley so I just went for it. They have similiar flavors and produce the creamy gelatinized starches that oatmeal does when cooked (although the oatmeal starches seem more silky and creamy than the flaked barley, and way more so than the dinkel).

I would have used more Vienna and even some Munich in the base profile (not a fan of only Pils as base), but I'm out! If I had used Munich and more Vienna, I would havep dropped the crystals a bit.

I also subbed in Caraamber for Victory. After tasting the CA grain it seemed more toasty/biscuity than sweet.... I note this because some think Weyermann CaraAmber is a crystal malt. I read about the debate today online and the Weyermann descriptions for this malt are poor and vague. After tasting though, I'm def of the opinion that it is more like Biscuit & Victory.

Preboil was at 14*Plato and only 20 liters or so, so i threw in a little brown sugar and DME to bring up the gravity, especially since I will top off 2-3 times during the boil (which was about 5 to 6 liters all together).

Official o.g. was 14P. About 21 liters in fermentor.. tasted great already! just a few points shy of target o.g., but maybe that helps keep the f.g. within reason.

Pitched rehydrated fresh pack of Safale US-05. fermenting in closet at 15 - 16*C ambient tempature.
