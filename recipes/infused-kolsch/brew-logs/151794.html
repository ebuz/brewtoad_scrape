Brewed by jll23

Brewing Notes

Mashing with about 1.5 gallons. ~.25 of that is distilled, the rest tap water with campden.

Will rinse the grains with about 2 gallons of campden-treated tap water.

Cooled the wort down to 63F before pitching it on the cake.

Overshot OG this time. Used the excuse to add a little more chilled distilled water for cooling. Left a decent amount of wort behind in the pot, along with the hop matter and break. This yeast does crop top a bit, so I wanted to make sure it had enough head space.

Temperature is set for 65F, ambiently. It's a little warmer than that after brewing, but I'm working to cool it down.

Threw a couple of hop pellets in at flame out. I never know if it really makes a difference, but I'd like to think there's just a bit of subtle hop aroma mixed in as a result.

Wort tasted sweet and grainy. Definitely get the pils malt in there (though I don't imagine the Kolsch malt is radically more intense).

Taste

At bottling, the sample is pale and slightly golden. Still pretty cloudy/yeasty. Tastes pretty great, though. Crisp, light malt. A little fruit flavoring from the yeast esters (maybe aided slightly by the hops). There are some sweet bready malt flavors, but they are on the periphery and the bitterness/dryness helps them remain a nice compliment to the beer. I have every reason to believe this is going to be another great German ale.

It has been about 2 weeks since bottling. Admittedly, I have cracked into a few of these before now. It's weird how excited understated German ales can get me sometimes. Anyway, this beer is a light, slightly golden color. Just darker than straw. Quite clear. Looks the part of a light beer though it's perhaps a touch darker than your typical American light lager (not that I'm complaining). I've always liked using Schill Kolsh malt before (it has a great depth of light malt flavor for a base malt) and I certainly like what I'm drinking here. That said, most of this beer was really Weyermann Barke Pils, so I'm a little unsure of which of these seemingly fantastic malts to give the credit to. What I will say is that malt presence in here, especially for a light beer, is fantastic. Some of that is also surely due to the trace amounts of Munich and Vienna from Oktale remnants in the yeast cake. That must add some complexity, but what really strikes me in this beer is how much the malt tastes like a well done German Pils. This is light German malt nailed. That's obviously the foundation of this beer style (or any delicate light colored beer coming from a German flavor tradition). It's not a very malty beer, but the malt that's there is grand if you take the time to notice it. It starts off just barely sweet, then it transitions to a drier biscuit flavor, and then ends with a bitterness that is just noticeable. Sure, there are some fruity esters in there, and small hints of German Select aroma, but those are embellishments to that general journey. The carbonation is, at least at this point, on the lower side (which I prefer to the alternative). There's just enough bubbles hanging around the outside of the glass. It's a damn attractive beer, and one I'm quite pleased with so far. Though I've downplayed its role so far, I should say that this German yeast strain is one of my favorites. It's fruitier/yeastier than WLP 029 (my other favorite), but the balance of complex German flavors is great. For the times you want a German ale with more expression from the yeast, it would be hard to do much better. Plus, it has dropped pretty bright in a short amount of time.

About a month in the bottle. Not a lot has changed since the last tasting notes were written, other than the beer perhaps getting a little less sweet as the carbonation increased. It's a really nice, grainy, light beer. In this bottle, at least, the beer is a little on the under-carbonated side, which I probably prefer. There's a bit of bubbles hanging around the glass, but it's not sparkling in the way a kolsch might be. I like the way the hops shine through for slight, dry bitterness. I also really enjoy the slight fruit from the yeast. Not a lot else to report, really. Really awesome, slightly bready, German ale.
