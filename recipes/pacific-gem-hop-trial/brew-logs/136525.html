Brewed by Brewcat

Brewing Notes

Getting used to using the refractometer. It seems to be important to let the liquid settle to room temperature, opening and closing the lid a few times and letting it sit. It makes a big difference, even if the liquid is not particularly hot. Doing this, I get much closer correlations with the hydrometer. The 'ATC' of the refractometer is not to be trusted.

Grainfather. Started mash at 13:10, 16L 66C. 90m, then smashed out at 76C for about 10 mins Pre-sparge 19.0 brix (1.075), 14:40 Post sparge (16.5L @75C) 12.0 brix (1.048) Boiled 25.5L, started 15:25 Turned off boil at 16:25, 22L, 14brix (1.057). Circulated wort through cooler to sanitise, then added 4.5L cold tap water to take the gravity down to 11.6brix (1.047). Temp still 85C so pasteurised. Confirmed with hydrometer (1.038@50C= 1.047@20C) Chilled wort to 20C in plastic fermenter, comes almost to top, about 25L. For some reason the OG measured (hydrometer 1.045, 11.1 brix (1.045)) is less than the measured gravity before chilling, despite temp compensation. Aerated as best I could and pitched packet of dry S05 yeast.

Calculated efficiency for 4.5Kg Pale ending in 26.5L @ 1.045 is 84%, about what I was expecting. However, by having to dilute it so much to hit the desired OG of 1.042, the hops have been thrown off, and will only be 25 IBU and not 30 as planned.

Fermentation Checks

Date Temperature Gravity Notes
08/01/2015 1:01am 18.0 degC 1.011

Took a wee peep; still lots of krausen against the lid so thought there was no point taking a hydrometer reading yet. 16-18Cs. However, refractometer gives 6.1 brix, 1.011. 4.5%
(http://www.northernbrewer.com/learn/resources/refractometer-calculator/), so maybe it is almost ready after all... Tastes OK, clean and malty with nice hop aroma. Very simple session beer.
08/05/2015 10:30pm 18.0 degC 1.007

Krausen has finally dropped, though still very cloudy. Big globs of yeast dropping of the lid. Racked to secondary to get it off all the superfluous yeast; will leave for another week to clear. Hydrometer reading finally possible: 1.007-8 (5%), refractometer 5.2 (1.006). Refractometer falls to 5.0 if you leave it sitting for half an hour!
08/16/2015 11:00am 18.0 degC 1.007

Kegged/bottled. 5.6 brix. All bubbles gone. Slightly cloudy still, tastes clean and nice. Agrees with FG hydrometer reading I took when I put it in secondary.
08/02/2015 3:04pm 18.0 degC 1.005

I've never come across this before: It's fermenting for 8 days, has fallen to 5.0 brix (1.005, 5.2% apparently) [subsequent readings have been 5.6 brix and hydrometer 1.007, so this must have been an anomaly] , but it still has a thick layer of krausen and yeast on the surface- Usually this would have dropped well before now, just leaving a thin layer of bubbles and no yeast. Guess I'll just wait until it does...

It smells OK, taste OK I think from the tiny sample I took for the reading. I've never made a beer this thin and simple before- possibly very slightly sour, but that might well just be the lack of sweetness and crystal that I'm used to. I've never used this dry Safale yeast before.
