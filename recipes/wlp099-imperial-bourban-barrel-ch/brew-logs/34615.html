Taste

Barrel tasting 10/5/2011 - pre dryhop
A - Dark as can be, this looks like an abyss...

S - Aroma is mild citrus and yeast with a gentle undertone of roasted malts from the chocolate malt.

T - Taste is citrus and bitterness with the citra hop opening up on the palate. Finishes with yeast. Not too much flavor from the chocolate and caramel malts here, just enough to where you can tell it was there in the recipe. Mostly contributing to color here. (yeasty)

M - It appears thin at the beginning, but then you get a good coating on the mouth. Very happy with the residual sugars in this one (finally).

O - A drinkable high gravity, but not finished yet. Need to vary the malts to achieve more balance in the flavor, maybe with more caramels and a touch of smoked malt for sweetness and depth while reducing the chocolate a little to compensate. Definitely a small amount of roasted barley for some of that good flavor, but careful on the ashen flavors...

11/21/2011 - Preliminary tasting

A - Dark as it could get. You barely even get any color on the outer edges when you hold it directly up to light. The head was quite large (over 3 inches?) and grew with the pour. The next one will have to be a half glass shared between two pints. I don't see any bubbles rising, but I am not sure I could if they were there.

S - The first smell is the bourbon barrel char chips VERY strong (less next time?) and some alcohol with the 21% showing through. It has a nice vanilla sweetness to it from the char chips too. There is a hint of hoppy floral note to it too, but it is hidden by bourbon, vanilla, and booze...
S - 6 months - Still very strong on the BBCC, but it melded a bit better after this aging period. The Chocolate Malt shows through mildly and nicely

T - The first taste is vanilla, then a mild citrus rind (tangerine and grapefruit). The finish seems to be bourbon and chocolate malt with the barleywine bitterness showing in the middle and the end. I might use the Citra hop again in the next batch, but I would consider others as well. The bitterness and citrus rind linger with the alcohol richness hanging on too.
T - 6 months - Milder this time, but still overpowering. I would say somewhere in the 14-16% max alcohol and 30% less BBCC next time. The Chocolate Malt showed through a little now, but the beer was screaming for Chocolate and Coffee to balance it.

M - The beer feels thicker than it is. It leaves a sugary coating eventually, but begins with a tingling carbonation and bitterness hitting the tongue. You feel this one throughout the mouth as it works its way though the entirety of sensations. It would be interesting with a noble hop or a Columbus hop in the mix to see how that would change the impression.

O - I think this is a very nice rendition for such an experiment. It is drinkable, yet I can tell the second bottle will age very well. I think this is a great start to my barleywine offering. It will be a work in progress for a couple of batches until I hit the right mix. Maybe the next one will be a one gallon size.

Next time: maybe a little less chocolate malt. english pale malt. a tiny bit of roasted barley could be good in it. Play with the hops a tiny bit, maybe retaining a hint of the citra character? Mild dryhopping with an aged EKG? it was a good finished sweetness, especially for aging.

Name suggestion: Hedy Lamarr/Hedley Lamarr (thx melly)
