Belgium Tripel

an All Grain Belgian Tripel by shamilton

  • Ingredients
  • 1 Brew Logs
  • 0 Variants
  • 0 Reviews
Print
Export BeerXML

Brewed by shamilton

On October 06, 2015

Brewing Notes

Brew day went pretty well. Ended up with a little more than 5.5 gallons, but only bc I didn't decant the yeast starter. Should have, but the yeast starter was a nightmare. Instead of stepping up, I made double at once and the yeast fermented like crazy out of the beaker and I don't know how much I lost. I don't think it was contaminated, but we'll see. Never do that again. Ever. I also added the sugar 10 minutes before flameout. Turned the flame off, poured it in slowly while continuously stirring. Good result.

Notes

Date Note
10/12/2015 10:28pm

So I transferred the beer into the secondary 6 days after brewing. The fermentation process was a nightmare. Stopped fermenting after 1 day. I swirled the entire bucket, it started fermenting like crazy, blew out the airlock on 3 separate occasions everywhere - cleaned everything up and re-sanitized the airlock...tasting the beer after 6 days, it actually does taste like a tripel. So the surprise was nice. Fermented down to 1.010 and with the krausen the way it was when I was transferring, I would say it has more to go. So, the fermentation. Started w/ one day @ 66 degrees. Then moved to 72. Blew out everywhere. Moved down to 62 for 2 days. Then back up to 72 for remainder - good luck mimicking that! haha. We'll see! Will add an extra week to secondary fermentation for a total of 3 weeks now! Then one week cold crash.

01/26/2016 5:10pm

So here is the interesting part of this beer. Even though it was cold crashed for so long…it still came out hazy. About a month in (half a key later) – it is one of the clearest beers I’ve ever made. The kicker – with the clarity came a loss of a lot of the Belgium yeast characteristics – to be expected though. Here is what I got:

Aroma: Spicy, like a hefeweizen – little banana, much more clove/spice; slight candy smell; NOT GETTING a lot of citrus though

Appearance: Gold, completely clear, very carbonated

Flavor: Definitely a light mouthfeel, almost something like a stronger coors, but not nearly at the ABV that it is. Sweet taste off the bat, a sweetness that lingers after the swallow (so it does not finish “very dry” like suggested). Definitely no hot alcohol taste, which is awesome for how strong this is. Lingering fruity taste in the back of the throat. Slight slight lemon with the swallow. You can detect, through the sweetness and the yeast flavor, if the beer is warm enough, the pilsner malt base, which is really cool! No getting a dry/bitter aftertaste though – and what is crazy is that this fermented all the way down to almost nothing…

Suggestions:
High carbonation is an absolute MUST. Give it the time it takes to carb. The clearer version of this beer, without the harshness of remaining yeast, is very drinkable. The yeast make it heavy, albeit flavor. I could have two or three of these. Definitely a better SUMMER beer. ***SERVE THIS BEER AT 50 DEGREES, nothing less! ***
Keep the turbinado…I like the taste!

Why isn’t there more Belgium lace???

If you really want to try something to change this recipe, maybe a different Belgium yeast. I like the flavor, not a die-hard fan, but it’s good. Not like Ladyface’s…I don’t know if I’ll ever figure that out. I feel like ladyface’s didn’t ferment down to a 1.010 FG…if that’s the case, you could always try to mash at 154. Or try 152 to “test the waters”
Either way, don’t fuck up the fermentation/yeast starter like you did. You’ll 100% have better results.
