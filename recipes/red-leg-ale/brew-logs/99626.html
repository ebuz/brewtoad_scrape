Brewed by Raven's Roost

On July 16, 2013

Brewing Notes

The slow cooling and rapid fermentation of this brew definitely produced some off flavors. Overall, the beer was drinkable, if not extremely enjoyable.

Next time I will avoid these issues by cooling the wort more quickly (at least with plastic tubing through an ice bath until I get a wort chiller) and controlling the temperature of the fermentation more carefully.

Notes

Date Note
07/16/2013 4:18am

I enjoyed a pint of Teamwork Ale while brewing in order to remember Aaron Kennison's (dharkhades) expert tutelage.
07/16/2013 4:24am

Troublesome Steps:

I forgot to stir as the mash was being brought to a boil. Some mash at the bottle of the brew kettle may have burned a little. A rough bottom was felt with the spoon.

I had difficulty keeping the wort at a boil without boiling over, especially immediately following adding the DME
07/16/2013 4:41am

Ingredients:

I used .75 cups of Grandma's gold standard, all natural, unsulphured molasses.

The hops (Northern Brewer) had an AAU of 10.9%

The yeast was pitched right from the pack. No starter was used. It was bagged on my birthday. June 13th, 2013
07/16/2013 4:43am

Questions:
Does the amount of time it takes to reach a boil matter for final taste/gravities?
07/16/2013 4:44am

Observations:

Boiling wort had a very distinct, deep, rich red coloring. (See fig 1.)

A lower than average OG was recorded (Fig 2). I need to be more consistent and accurate with my saccharification rest.

My thermometer crapped out on me during the chilling phase. I was unable to measure the temp of the wort when the yeast was pitched.

I added 2.9 gallons to the bottling bucket to chill the wort. A very small temp decrease was noted. Ice needed for next batch, topping off is insufficient to chill wort.

The chilling of the wort was insufficient (Fig 3). Next time I will try longer tubing with ice added to the sink at the very least.
