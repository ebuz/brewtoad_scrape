Brewed by propush

On March 07, 2015

Brewing Notes

Messy messy day.

double batch brew - same beer different yeast. Was supposed to end up with 2X21L wort. One is wlp800, the other is w3470. The wlp had 1.5L starter after harvest.

Ended up with 24L in one (including starter), and then a sad 15.5L in the other.

AFTER ADDING YEAST TO THE 24L, TRANSFERED 1L of wort the the small container.

Ended up with 23L (including 1.5L starter) in the wlp fermenter, and 16.5 including 1L mixed with wlp in the other. Supposed to be also yeast experiment, but what ever, the WLP are less in number, and it is just 6.5% of the volume so I don't think Ill feel that.

Also messed up hops. I had recipe say 30 min addition of hops, but all over the net it is 15. So... I shorted the boil 10 minutes. so first addition at 50. second at 20. third at 5.

To learn: Notice what you put in the container. Never add yeast to a double batch before you are done. Batch used 40L for sparge and mash + 10L~ (eyeballed it) and it was missing about 2L. The kettle was almost almost overflowing. Only chance is to add the 2L in the last second next time.

38L wort @ 1.060 (would have been fixed if hit 44L planned wort). Placed in 9C ambient (to hit ~10c fermentation) at 17:00, Saturday 7.3.15.

Notes

Date Note
03/10/2015 7:35pm

After 24 hours the wlp 800 was fermenting and the w3470 was lagging. 2 days in both had krausen. But only wlp 800 had airlock activity. I have checked and the buckets are closed. but still no airelock activity in the w3470 - maybe leaky bucket, maybe it is how it is. Fermentation of wlp 800 smells like yeast dough.

Tasted both (72 hours in). Tastes worty still, both taste the same I think. Highly carbonated.

Decided to up the temp to 10 ambient. I do not think much heat is produced in there at this slow rate and cold temps. And I under-pitched the WLP800, so might as well give them this 1 single degree.
03/15/2015 2:07pm

Sunday 8th day of fermentation. I have taken sample from both.
WLP 800 tastes worty and is 1.030, left in chamber.
W3470 is at 1.020 which is nearing finish. It has 70% attenuation only so it should hit 1.018. If it were to hit 75% then that would become about 1.015. Anyways, should be more then 80% done. The sample formed a little bit of Krausen - So it is still fermenting. Moved to room temp for di-acetyl rest.
03/17/2015 11:12pm

Moved the wlp800 to d-rest. Did not measure gravity again, just let it have two more days from the 1.030, with 2 more C in temp - and now room temp for d -rest.

Both buckets will go to cold conditioning in about 3 weeks, because there are two batches of ale to ferment first.
03/25/2015 11:28pm

the w4370 was placed into laggering today. it seems like 12L tops. moved it bucket twice because it was full of hops, then tried to pour some CO2 over before closing the lid.

04/19/2015 7:52pm

moved the wlp800 batch to lagering
