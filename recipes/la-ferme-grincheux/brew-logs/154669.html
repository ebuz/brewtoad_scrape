La Ferme Grincheux

an All Grain Belgian Specialty Ale by jll23

Brewed by jll23

Brewing Notes

Mashing with a little over 2 gallons. ~.5 of that is distilled water and the rest is tap water treated with campden.

Will "sparge" with a little over 2 gallons of campden-treated tap water.

Will add a handful of rice hulls at the end of the mash to aid in draining the grains.

Boil volume is going to be a little over 4 gallons. My pot is small and I don't want to push it much more than that.

Cooled the wort down to about 80F and pitched both smack packs. Loosely covered the front of the carboy in a blanket and put it in the back of a closet. Supposed to get pretty hot in a few days, so I'm assuming this one will be working in the higher 80's at least.

Pretty massive overshoot on the gravity. Not sure why, though I did mash as low as I have at this location.

Priming pretty conservatively at bottling. I've had a lot of gushers this summer and none of them had brett or bacteria in them.

Hydrometer read 1.006, but corrected it since beer temp was somewhere around 80F.

Taste

Wort sample was light and sweet. Tasted a touch like malt grain, and I definitely got wheat flavor. Even though I used a tiny amount of hops, I could smell them in the wort. They weren't strong, but it was easy to attribute any aromas that weren't grain-based to them.

At bottling, it's a nice yellow color. There is a slight but noticeable sourness to the beer. I smell/taste the wheat graininess. There's a good bit of saison character in terms of esters and subtle phenolics. They go pretty well with the little bit of sourness, which somehow makes it seem more like wine. All in all, things are still quite subdued with this one, but the core flavors bode well for aging.

Tasting one of these about three weeks in bottle. It has flavors of both your typical saison yeast, sourness/acidity, and a bit of nutty grain (from the wheat?). I did try one of these a week ago and it was still a sour farmhouse ale, but in a bland way. This one was a lot better, suggesting that the flavors are melding over time. The sourness is pretty good already, it's just a matter of how it works with the rest of the beer. Anyway, I am thinking things are on track after this last one.

It's about 3+ months in bottle, almost 4 since brewing. The beer has good carbonation at pouring, but poor head retention. It becomes still after a few minutes. Nothing to hold the gas there. Fair enough, that's a sour for you. There are still a number of nice, flavorful saison compounds in this beer, but they degrade further with each week or so. Instead, the nutty, funky compounds increase in intensity. But again, there is no brett in here to fill in the gaps between the saison yeast and the bacteria. The saison declines, the sourness, which is light and airy, but ascendant can only do so much as a feature. I realize that as a "sour beer" this one is still young, but for the moment it seems to just be getting weirder. Maybe some magic will become of this, but to my tastes it's getting farther away from the eclectic magic you might want in your Belgian beer and more towards the one dimensional oddity. That said, the subtleties of it are still really nice, it's just not nearly as subtly complex as the best sours. I don't think I'll ever have a homebrew aging for an entire year--that seems crazy to me--but maybe I can squirrel away a few of these. The problem always is that everything good about my homebrews degrades quite rapidly after bottling, and I'm not sure how in the world the quality of the ingredients is suddenly going to reappear after excessive aging. A sour like this might be the exception to that rule, but the psychological hurdles remain the same. Anyway, this beer remains a perfectly interesting one, with well executed lactic sourness. Any identity it had, however, is currently being broken down by the bacteria. Strong saison aromas (which I thought worked out pretty well in the initial beer) are being warped into less pleasant versions of themselves. I'm getting less DuPont panoply and more iffy single renegade notes throughout the beer. Again, I hope it might eventually resume rounding out (like I thought it was going to and it seemed to be doing for the first few months), but right now, while still good and complex, this thing is tasting a little awkward.
