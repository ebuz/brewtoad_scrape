Knicker Kicker

an All Grain Fruit Beer by clayzermk1

  • 2 Brew Logs
  • 0 Variants
  • 0 Reviews

Export BeerXML

Brewed by clayzermk1

On September 06, 2015

Notes

Date Note
09/06/2015 3:15am

wiped stir plate clean, placed upstairs in office on desk and verified heating was off

OxyClean then StarSan cleaned funnel and flask, covered with napkin, turned upside down to drain/dry
StarSan cleaned stir bar, noticed metal filings on bar, wiped clean, 91%IPA cleaned stir bar, air dried, placed in flask and re-covered the flask

removed yeast and KS apple juice from kegerator
placed 3/4 KS apple juice in main fridge (~38F)
shook yeast vial and placed on top shelf of main fridge temporarily

KS apple juice: 77.0F, 11.7Brix

vial of yeast smelled of white bread and King's Hawaiian sweet rolls
yeast lot number: 1018359
tested 7/20/2015 9:05am, pH 4.38, cell count 2.33, viability 99.6%

poured 1000ml apple juice into flask via funnel
added yeast vial to flask
covered with aluminum foil
placed on stir plate set to about 3.25 speed
covered with brown packing paper
ambient temp in room is 79F
09/06/2015 3:50pm

Yeast starter looking good. Ambient air temperature is 73F.
09/06/2015 11:41pm

Added:
remainder of the gallon of apple juice I used for the yeast starter to the kettle, covered, and set to boil
2ea cinnamon sticks
1.974lbs (meas) (1ea 2lb bag) of brown sugar
0.138lbs of Ojai Valley Bee Farm raw orange blossom honey
0.564lbs of d'arbo naturrein blossom honey
1.55lbs of Julian Cider Mill orange and white sage honey
cut off heat, left covered
09/07/2015 1:41am

OxyClean cleaned kegs and all parts. Put on new gaskets.

Turned off stir plate.
09/07/2015 4:44am

Boiled wort while cleaning kegs.

Put fresh gaskets on keg parts. Cleaned with OxyClean, rinsed with tap, cleaned with StarSan.

Final wort is 19.8Brix, 80F. Sampled with vodka cleaned pipette.
Shook yeast starter, poured through funnel into keg.
Rolled on ground between myself and MattO for about 5min to aerate.
Finished about 10pm.
09/08/2015 4:00am

Around 9pm, I placed the keg into the kegerator and hooked up the thermostat set to ~68F.
09/08/2015 3:43pm

This morning I checked on the kegerator-turned-fermentation chamber, the temp had dropped to 54F. I tested the thermostat, when the set point is higher than the temp the fridge kicks on. This is backwards and should be that if the temp is higher than the set point the fridge kicks on. I switched the operation of the thermostat and set it to ~72F.

The keg had a lot of pressure so I relieved it until krausen spurted out the top a bit. Need to get a blowoff tube.
09/08/2015 8:20pm

Increased thermostat temp from 72F to 75F.

Sanitized gas ball lock and hose taken from disassembled picnic faucet. Sanitized top of keg and quick connects.
Poured half a pint of sanitizer into a pint mason jar, inserted blowoff tube, connected blowoff tube.
Pressure was so great that it ejected from the blowoff jar. I managed to grab it and insert it back into the blowoff jar in time for the krausen to come rolling through. The tube boiled for a good minute or two, spilling some into the driveway. Once it settled down, I disconnected the blowoff tube and sanitized the tube along with the top of the keg. I rinsed the blowoff jar and added another half pint of sanitizer. I placed the keg in the kegerator and attached the blowoff assembly. The blowoff jar is sitting in a small bowl to catch spillage.

Lesson learned: use a blowoff tube from the get go.

Placed a 22floz beer bottle of water in the kegerator to come up to temp to use for a wet bulb temp reading.
09/08/2015 11:41pm

Blowoff is bubbling. Placed thermostat bulb into water bottle. Decreased thermostat temp from 75F to 70F.
09/09/2015 3:34pm

Blowoff bubbling has slowed slightly. Kegerator air temp is 72F.
09/10/2015 7:42pm

Blowoff is bubbling. Kegerator air temp is 68F.
09/15/2015 7:28pm

Blowoff is bubbling about once every 1.5-2s. Kegerator air temp is 73F.

Yesterday, I purchased 3ft of black beer line and an extra 1/4in barb assembly. I should have purchased an extra beer quick disconnect as well.

Today I cut ~18in from the 3ft length, sanitized it, sanitized the picnic faucet (disassembled), sanitized the 1/4in barb, sanitized the two SS hose clamps from the old picnic faucet assembly, and sanitized the beer quick disconnect (disassembled). I reassembled a new homebrew picnic faucet for sampling the fermenting product. I placed the assembly between two paper towels to dry.
09/16/2015 7:03pm

Blowoff is bubbling about once every 2.5s. Kegerator air temp is 73F. Decreased thermostat temp from 70F to 67F.
09/17/2015 7:06pm

Blowoff is bubbling about once every 2s. Kegerator air temp is 72F (despite new lower thermostat setting, thermometer 24hr low is 63F).
09/21/2015 7:40pm

Blowoff is bubbling about once every 2-3s. Kegerator air temp is 66F.
09/26/2015 6:00pm

Blowoff is bubbling about once every 3-3.5s. Kegerator air temp is 70F.
10/02/2015 4:07am

Blowoff is was not bubbling. Kegerator air temp is 73F.
10/02/2015 4:17am

Sampled cider. Temp was 67.5F. Brix is 9.5.

Poured a couple ounces into a jar to get the brunt of the yeast cake.

Poured a couple more ounces into a clean glass.
V: cloudy straw, small amounts of yeast
A: spice, alcohol
T: tart, herby, apple skin residual
M: balanced to dry
O: less than I was hoping for, dryness perhaps helps this batch out a bit, the aroma and flavor are lacking
