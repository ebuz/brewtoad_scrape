Knicker Kicker

Brewed by clayzermk1

On November 13, 2015

Notes

Date Note
11/13/2015 5:39pm

Purchased yeast 5:20pm 11/12/2015 from WL. Transported in insulated bag with ice pack. Placed upright in kegerator by thermostat probe. Kegerator thermostat is set to 40F.

WLP028
MFG: Oct 13, 2015
Lot #: 1020516
Test Date: 10/9/2015 7:24:09AM
pH: 3.74
Cell Counting: 1.86E9cells/ml * 35ml/vial = 65.1E9cells/vial
Viability Test: 99.60%

Assuming initial viability, pitch rate would be 65100E6cells / 15141.6ml / 19.8P = 0.21714209778. Target is 1.0.
Since the yeast is 30 days old, the viability is about 78%. 65.1E9cells/vial * 0.78 = 50.778E9cells/vial.
Estimated initial pitch rate is 50778E6cells / 15141.6ml / 19.8P = 0.16937083627.
Assuming 50.78E9cells, 1.25L starter volume, 11.7P apple juice for the starter wort, and using Braukaiser's model:
Growth Rate: 1.4
Intial Cells Per Extract (B/g): 0.30
Ending Cell Count: 286 billion cells
Resulting Pitch Rate: 0.95M cells / mL / °P

Target brew time is 3-4pm tomorrow. Should allow 24hrs for the starter.
11/14/2015 3:18am

Washed funnel.

Sanitized funnel, flask, and stir bar. Let funnel, flask, and stir bar air dry. Stir bar is suspended inside upturned flask with a heavy magnet.

Measured KS apple juice at 12P (just a hair below the 12 line), 66F.

Washed, flamed, and alcoholed scissors. Alcoholed top of yeast "vial" (plastic bag tube thing).
Righted flask and wiped rim with alcohol.
Placed the funnel in the top of the flask.
Shook yeast vial gingerly until well mixed.
Cut open the top of the yeast vial. Some of the yeast in the vial spurted out when I cut it and ran down the non-sanitized portion of the bag into the funnel below >:(
Yeast smells of white bread and faintly of apples.
Poured in ~1.3L of KS apple juice.
Covered the top with aluminum foil.
Placed upstairs on stir plate set to 4 and covered in blanket.
Placed remainder of apple juice in the fridge (all 3+gal are in the fridge).

11/14/2015 5:41pm

Stir bar had destabilized sometime during the night. There was decent yeast sediment before manually swirling and correcting the stir bar. Set the stir plate to 3.5 since that seemed to work last time.
11/15/2015 3:01am

Added remainder of sub-1gal KS apple juice to kettle.
Added:
1.018lbs KS organic honey
0.996lbs L.R. Rice Raw and Unfiltered Honey
2.014lbs Dark Brow C&H sugar (used dark instead of golden per me)
4ea cinnamon sticks (doubled cinnamon sticks per VW)

Set on high center burner to boil for 30min.
11/15/2015 4:17am

Boiled wort for 30min.
Sanitized funnel.
Added 3gal cold KS apple juice.
Poured starter into keg.
Wort measured 26.3P at 74F. Covered with lid.

Aerated wort by kicking around for ~5min.

Added sanitized blow off tube and jar of sanitizer to kegerator. Attached to keg.
Set thermostat to 65F.
11/15/2015 8:15pm

Last night, I accidentally put the air coupler on the beer port. I pried it off and swapped the ports thinking I had them on the wrong sides. I then plugged the air coupler on the beer tube port. The CO2 from the fermenting cider pushed the cider out the beer tube. After about 12hrs, all but maybe a quart or so of the cider was spilled onto the floor of the garage.

I cleaned everything off. Swapped the beer and air ports again so they are on the correct tubes. Sanitized them and reattached the blow off tube. They are air drying in the garage.
05/07/2016 6:38pm

Took up the last 2 qts of cider. The kegerator was set to 40°F. Drinkable. 9.3°P. Boozy and dry. Cinnamon aroma. Quite good!

Between 9.8% and 11.2% ABV.
