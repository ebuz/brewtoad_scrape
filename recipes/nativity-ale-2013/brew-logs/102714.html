Nativity Ale 2013

an All Grain Belgian Dark Strong Ale by Churchyard

Brewed by Churchyard

On September 09, 2013

Fermentation

Measured Original Gravity
1.070
Measured Final Gravity
1.013
ABV
7.5%

Age & Package

Priming Sugar
Sucrose (Table Sugar)

Notes

Date Note
09/08/2013 10:09pm

Well, that didn't go as planned.

For whatever reason (maybe because I was multi-tasking too much), I trusted f-ing brewtoad which said I need to pull off 9 gallons of wort. As a result, I wound up with seven gallons instead of five, and my OG was thrown way off.

I also had too much wort, so I pitched a couple packs of Saison Dupont yeast on the remainder. (3 packs of 1214 were pitched @ 68°)

Quad? Probably not. 1.070 is too low for a Quad. Dubbel? Certainly.

Still going to pitch one gallon on sour cherries. I also intend to save 2 gallons worth of bottles for a year and taste them next Christmas.

Another brewday...
09/20/2013 2:49pm

The temp in the brew fridge was dipping under 70°. The saison listed it as 68° (dubbel 66°), the former of which was corroborated by the in-fridge thermometer. Because of the high likelihood of the saison stalling (which may not even be a problem given that I'm adding brett), and the fact that 1214 enjoys higher temps anyhow, I kept the thermostat the same (72°) but added my heating element (first being sure to cover the carboys to protect from light). I'll leave the light on up to bottling.
10/03/2013 7:07pm

Racked the Saison version and pitched White Labs Lambicus (WLP653), 3/4 oz oak chips, week old dregs from Jolly Pumpkin Baudelaire, and fresh dregs from both a 2012 & 2013 Fantome Hiver, Hill Farmstead Edith, and The Commons Flemish Kiss. The bad news is that I don't have as much of this as I thought: there is a full gallon of head space in my carboy. Not sure there's anything I can do about it. I will have to be super vigilant over this one.

Oh, and I forgot to taste it first!

Tasting notes on the dregs:

Hiver is crazy funky. The 2013 tasted like manure and marijuana with a burnt cigar finish. The 2012 is more smoky though much more balanced.

Flemish Kiss is like a pale and mild version of a Flemish Red. Super drinkable and pretty mellow.

Edith is a black saison, with a bit of sour bite, a little more acidic, but perfectly rounded.
10/04/2013 6:18pm

Took my first sample of the full 1214 version. It's definitely not bad, but also rather underwhelming. Aromas of chocolate, bananas, and clove.

The banana isn't nearly as strong as it was in my maple tripel (thank God!), though it's definitely still there. The chocolate malt cuts through a little more than i would have liked, though it does lend a fantastic color. It is strange how the chocolate malt aroma is much stronger in the saison version... and I think it works better, too.

It is *way* more balanced than the second runnings version. I think that Pomegranate molasses is to blame!

Is this a dubbel? Maybe, maybe not. Drinks more like a Belgian Bruin, I think.

I think it would do well with some lagering before bottling; might round out the flavors a little bit. I'm going to ponder for a while the best course of action. I think I'm going to split it 2:3, two gallons getting lagered for three weeks and then bottled (in two one-gallon fermentors), three gallons going to cherries, brett, and oak. I will then age the bottles until december, then drink the majority in the spring and next year.
10/07/2013 12:25am

With only two gallons of wort in the three gallon brett fermentor, I decided to rack off a gallon of the dubbel and fill up the Saison to the top. So it will now be a 2:1 mixture/blend, but I think that will be fine. And that's better than the alternative: acidic beer.

I then racked one gallon of the dubbel onto raisins and brett Lambicus. I wanted to rack onto cherries, but I could not find frozen tart cherries anywhere. So raisins it was. I did not sanitize them, but simply put them directly into the fermentor.

The remaining gallons were siphoned into my big glass jar for lagering. It was not filled all the way and will likely experience some oxidation. But so be it.

My bigger concern was that the dubbel suddenly tasted like plastic. It was awful: tons of off flavors. Not sure how that would have happened in the span of two days; my hope is that it is just way less refined than what I was drinking previously: a 3-yr old Judgement Day (Lost Abbey). We'll find out in a couple weeks.
10/17/2013 3:10pm

Yowza. Opened up the brewfridge today and found a shit-ton of beer all over the place. Apparently the Belgian yeast had a hey-day with the raisins. Duh.

Anyhow, added a blow off tube as it still seems pretty active. Gonna have to clean that thing up at some point.
10/18/2013 8:14pm

Bottled 21 bottles.
