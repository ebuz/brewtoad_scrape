Brewed by stuartgrant

On October 27, 2014

Brewing Notes

Pitching approx 150 billion cells Wyeast French Saison (3711), plus the slurry from a very active 1L starter of wild lactobacillus cultured from grain. I will ferment at 25°C.

Fermentation Checks

Date Temperature Gravity Notes
10/27/2014 5:30am 26.0 degC 1.040

[0 days] Racked off cold break 2 hours after chilling, with plenty of splashing but no active aeration. Pitched dregs of 1L starter of Lacto plus approx 150 billion cells French Saison yeast (slight underpitch). Applied heat belt 3 hours later.
+12 hours: maintaining 26°C with small krausen visible.
10/31/2014 8:03am 25.0 degC 1.005

[4 days] Put lid on fermenter after 3 days. Definitely slowed down. Measured pH at 4.14, so still no obvious action from lactobacillus. Flavour is much fruitier and cleaner today, with just a hint of the floral/lactic note I recognise from the Berliner ... however this could just be the 3711.
11/06/2014 9:52am 28.0 degC 1.004

[10 days] Added dry hops and switched off heat belt. pH reading was 4.19, same as yesterday, and SG was marginal between 3 and 4, so it's definitely done. Will keg/bottle this in 4 days' time.
11/10/2014 9:30am 18.0 degC 1.004

[14 days] Kegged 19L plus 5 x 750mL bottles. Heaps of suspended hop particles - I hope this isn't a problem in the keg. Hop flavour and aroma were great. Keg got 1.2L reserved wort for 4g/L CO2 (I'll force carb up to 5-6g/L), while each bottle got 2 x carb drops. Also added 1tsp gelatin dissolved in water to the keg, but not sure what effect this will have given that it's going to be at room temp for the next 2-3 weeks.
01/17/2015 6:34am 21.0 degC 1.002

[82 days] Heaps of gas needed releasing, and 2 point drop in SG. However, the pH is 4.27 so definitely zero sourness. Flavour seems to be cleaning up a little though, with an interesting fruitiness from the hops.
11/09/2014 9:10pm 17.0 degC 1.004

[13.5 days] Planning to bottle tonight. Strangely, the pH has risen again to 4.27. However, there is some tartness in flavour, maybe accentuated by dry hops and cooler temperature. Lacto doesn't seem to have done much at all. But should be tasty anyway.
11/05/2014 7:10am 28.0 degC 1.004

[9 days] pH has risen to 4.19 for some reason. Sample was very cloudy - think it was a glob of yeast in the tap - so I'll test again tomorrow. Heat belt is definitely keeping the temp up.
10/29/2014 9:20pm 27.0 degC 1.008

[2.5 days] Open fermentation proceeding well. Heat belt has been on most of the time, with temperature hovering around 26°C most of the time. Seeing as it's nearly finished, I will close the fermenter tonight and leave it to attenuate (and sour) with heat belt for the remainder of the week.

Taste is great - sweet and fruity, with only a slight sourness in the finish. Hard to detect any obvious influence from the Lactobacillus yet, but pH is 4.15 which seems pretty low. It might be a case of getting sort slowly over time.
