Date Note
12/06/2013 7:43pm

This is the first beer that I have brewed. Some may say, "Why so low on the IBU for an IPA?". That's because I'm not a fan of IPAs. "Well then why brew an IPA"? That's because it was part of a kit from Northern Brewer on Black Friday. I decided to make lemons from lemonade and do a little experimenting right off the bat. With some assistance from TheBenner, we modified the recipe just a little bit to remove most of the hops and hopefully come up with something a long the lines of a blonde. Who knows...

12/14/2013 5:23am

Today I transferred to the secondary. It was my first time trying a transfer. I lost a few pints in the bottom that the siphon wouldn't pick up. I could have tried to get them, but I was worried about sucking a bunch of trub into the secondary so I called it a loss. Specific gravity leaving the primary was a 1.016, maybe a 1.014. That calculates out to an ABV of 6.04% to 6.30%. I didn't screw up this time and forget to get a taste. Being somewhat of a beer describing noob all I can say is that it tastes like I was hoping for. It starts off tasting like an IPA, you get the citrus, but you don't finish off with any of the bitter that I dislike in IPAs. It's not overly sweet, which was a big worry.
12/16/2013 11:31pm

Beer has turned a very, very dark brown from its golden color.
12/27/2013 11:00pm

Today I transferred to the keg. The final gravity was 1.013. That gives a final ABV of 6.43%. I force carbed at around 20psi and placed it in the kegerator for a couple hours before first sample.
12/28/2013 1:29am

Beer is cold, first glass was poured. I'm not even sure how to describe it. It tastes pretty good. I'll update after a half dozen more glasses or so. :)
