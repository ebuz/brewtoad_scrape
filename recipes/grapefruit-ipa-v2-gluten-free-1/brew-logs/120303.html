Taste

Brew day: the wort tasted sweet and bitter. There was no real sorghum twang* only the sweetness of the fermentables with a distinct hop bitterness (even more bitter than v1 if my memory serves correct here). *Overall, this is what I was hoping for. We will see in a few weeks after fermentation is complete how we did.

Notes

Date Note
06/18/2014 1:00am

The brew day went fairly smooth but as always I throw myself curve balls due to poor planning:

-I made some last minute field adjustments to the recipe. I found I had only 23 oz of buckwheat honey left so rather than use on 16 oz and leave 7-8 oz in the tub, I used 23 oz and reduced the sorghum extract by a similar amount that got me to the same SG and same SRM (which was 1.75 pounds of WSE, reduced from 2lbs recipe.) Coupled with the introduction of the Brown Rice Syrup, we may have a reduced sorghum twang (hopefully).

-Somehow I miscalculated the amount of hops on hand and didn't have the 0.5 oz of Cascade at 5 minutes I needed. Instead, I used 0.5 oz of Amarillo. I found that these can be a substitute in a pinch. I may have a slightly different hop profile but it shouldn't be any worse/better, only different? I'm just glad that I had other hops in my freezer to use in a pinch. I know feel the need to keep hops on hand, stockpiled in a sense, for these brew days. The days of buying hops for each recipe are fading and the days of me having a freezer with a small supply of various hops is approaching. Kind of cool, I feel like I'm graduating out of newbie brew school in a way.

Since I have another batch in secondary in a bucket I found myself in a bit of a jam on where to rack the wort into primary. So there was a bit of a snafu racking from kettle to primary: first I used the valve on the kettle to dump most of the wort into the bucket which worked great especially with the new paint strainer bag** on top of the bucket. I then realized that I didn't have an extra lid with a drilled hole on my backup bucket, so I racked that to a better bottle, only to realize I was damn near the top with little headspace and I expect the us-05 yeast to be powerful fermenters for this batch and I didn't want a mess, so... yep... dumped it back into the bucket, removed the lid from the batch which has an airlock, put a non-drill sanitized lid loosely on my Strawberry Blond in secondary, then cleaned and sanitized the drilled lid and sealed it onto this batch. Well, at least the wort was well aerated!

**With the high sedimentation of these fermentables, and the usual hop trub, I found that some folks where using paint strainer bags to put over their buckets. i picked up 2 from Ace and I dumped chilled wort from kettle to bucket.

This morning, no bubbling yet, but I expect it to any moment. if it doesn't I'll check the temps and move it to a warmer spot if needed.

Kegged around 14 July 2014. As of 6 Aug, the beer is starting to get nice, but I think it still needs a few weeks to get into its prime around mid-late Aug 2014.
