Brewing Notes

Taste

At bottling, the malt is really nice. Kind of honaey-like. Not super sweet with the bitterness. Not a lot of hop flavor so far, which is fine. A little bit of spiciness is in there too, which I'm interested to see what will become of over time.

Cracked into this a couple of weeks after bottling. It's got a lot of nice light malt flavor. Definitely some spiciness from the Vienna. Nice clean German yeast taste. In combination, it's got some bready/biscuit flavors. There's just a little hoppiness to lend a hint of fruit. Dry enough to be about right on with this level of bitterness. Nice and lager-like.

It's been a few more weeks, and while I still like the beer, I keep going back and forth on whether it is a little too dry for this combination of bitterness and carbonation or if it's just a little to bland/sweet. The flavors really seem to die at the end. That concerns me. Otherwise, what I said previously remains right on.

Right, so it's over a month in bottle. I'm going to use this opportunity to talk about the hops, because, though I have used them in at least 4 other beers, this one contained the least amount of aroma/flavor additions and still suffers representative difficulties of all the rest.

In principle, I really like this version of the Hallertau line ("Tradition"). It's got some interesting fruit. In practice, having used it in a few beer, I really don't care for it. Hops should be something to balance out malt, not weirdly enforce sickly sweet fruit aromas that are neither sought out or asked for. I just want a lot more from a noble-ish hop, and the fact that there are nearly no actual late addition hops in this beer hits the flavor problems for this hop even more. In small doses, I kind of like the fruit combinations in the aroma, but again, hops aren't just flavoring--they're supposed to be bitter and anchoring. These just don't seem to do those later, more important roles.

Some of my other beers are even worse for the wear when it comes to these particular hop flavor additions, but this one should have been virtually untouched by aroma and still has all these all these sweet flavors that I really struggle with.

This beer had a lot of promise. It was fairly dry, using possibly my favorite German ale yeast. Simple as could be. A bit malty, with nice spice. Sadly, there are a lot of too-sweet fruit flavors that I cannot quite get behind.

Killing it off, about 2 months after bottling. Very nice malt with spice and creaminess. Haven't loved the hops, but they basically support the creaminess right now. Malt is really nice. Would love to try this again with other hops. The basis of a masterpiece, one imagines.
