Imperial S'more

an All Grain Russian Imperial Stout by Daniel Slaughter

Brewed by Daniel Slaughter

On January 03, 2015

Notes

Date Note
01/03/2015 9:00pm

Mash temperature: 156.2 F
First runnings: 1.091/1.088
Second runnings: 1.044
Pre-boil: 1.081
OG: 1.096

Daniel C. assisted with the brew day.

Only added 2oz of Cocoa Powder (Nestle) instead of 4oz. Will see cocoa level at 2nd fermentation and possibly add 2oz more.

Runnings tasted well balanced; light on smoke, very sweet, not too much caramel, nice roasted flavor. Nothing seemed to drastically over power the flavor. If anything it resembled a campfire's singed wood. I didn't taste the wort after the boil, so I'm not sure what the cinnamon, cocoa, vanilla, hops, or boil added to it.

We had extra second runnings at 1.044, so instead of adding it all to the batch and diluting it we took the excess (about 1.5L) and treated it as a yeast starter. It will be interesting to see how this turns out, as there was no boil. We added 2 hop pellets of both Tettnanger and Galena to it, as well as the same yeast.
01/11/2015 7:58pm

Current gravity: 1.026

I've been fermenting this at 68 up until last night when I turned the furnace back onto its 63/68 schedule.

Throughout the entire fermentation I never saw any bubbles from the blow off tube. There always did seem to be pressure under the lid, which if I pushed on would then cause bubbles in the jug filled with sanatizer. Today I switched the blow off tube to a bubbler, checked the gravity rating (1.026) and took a sample.

The gravity rating of 1.026 is quite surprising that the yeast brought it down to 1 point away from its FG already. I imagine it may drop 1-2 more points before I keg it and let it sit for 6+ months.

I tasted the sample warm. It's a very nice stout. I don't get much smokeyness (maybe subtlety). The vanilla and cinnamon are lost, but I question if their characteristics do play a role in the final flavors. Malt wise this is very balanced, and I'm not tasting any characteristic that is too overpowering (maybe a little bit of burnt). There is a strong alcohol burn (but not terrible), and bitterness from the hops (which I wish was less). I'm thinking the cinnamon, vanilla, and smoke flavor will probably come out as that diminishes with time. It has a rich mouth feel, and I don't get overpowering sweeness, but my lips and mouth feel sugar coated sometime after drinking it. I'm not so sure it is bad enough to try and correct it.

I left the sample in the freezer for 15 minutes. There was a haze before, but after letting it sit that has cleared up nicely. I assume there is still active yeast having their way with the beer. While swishing it around in my mouth I'm getting a lot of black currants, raisins, vodka burn, and burnt sugar (toffee, coffee, brown sugar). I only drank 1/4cp or so, and I feel a warming sensation from the alcohol.

The aftertaste is interesting, it reminds me of something. It almost tastes like baked sesame seeds (maybe that's the hops) on the front of my tongue. When breathing through my nose I get dark chocolates. On the back of my tongue I get bananas.

There's not a lot to the aroma other than alcohol. When I first popped the lid off, I did get a whoosh of bananas that quickly went away.

This should be a good beer.
02/28/2015 5:00am

Kegged, bottled 4.
12/31/2016 5:00pm

Tapped the keg. It's good! It has a rich dark cloudy/murky color, similar to a very dark chocolate milk. It smells and tastes slightly boozy, but isn't harsh. Some of the smoked malt comes through, followed by a slight bit of bourbon.

Next time I'd triple the amount of bourbon in it, and drop the caramel malts just a bit.
