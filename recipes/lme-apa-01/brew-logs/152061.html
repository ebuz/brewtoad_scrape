LME APA 01

a Partial Mash American Pale Ale by Robert

Brewed by Robert

On November 19, 2017

Brewing Notes

Batch #20. Using some new equipment. Gonna ferment in the glass 1.4 gallon big mouth bubbler. Gonna steep in the new fine mesh nylon bag (17x20). Gonna use the old plastic and metal strainer in the kitchen drawer for straining the wort as it goes into the fermentor. Grain mill still working great. Used the strainer to pre-strain the last of the grain to remove the dust. Worked so well on the honey malt I went back and did the other malts. Gets rid of the dust at the bottom of the plastic yogurt containers which hold the malts. Boiled 13 pints for the steep and the kettle is looking almost full! Should add about 1 ounce per ounce of specialty grain (8oz for this batch) to cover losses in steeping. The grain sucks up some of the water and doesn't let go of it. New steeping bag worked great and was easy to clean by just turning it inside out and washing it after all the cooled grain got dumped out. Mmm smells good. Measured out the LME into a measuring cup and added the DME and Whirlfloc all before the boil so much less moisture in the air so a lot less problems and stickiness to deal with. Added 0.07 oz of Citra Hops for a total of 0.17oz FWH. No messes or mistakes so far so I'm getting back into my brewing groove again. This batch will probably be the bulk of the Christmas beer plus maybe a 4 bottle IPA run and maybe a 4 or 8 bottle run testing out the Extra Light LME with an even lighter hop load (blond ale?). Last of the horizon hops. I didn't like the pellet hops overall since I don't have any fine filtering going on and they don't look or smell as good as the whole hops from the LBS. I got some Cascade hops to replace them but will have to use about twice as much to get equal bitterness. I can also use some Citra hops which are high AA as well. So many hops so few brew days. Overdid the hops a bit (again!) but I have trouble putting them back in the foil pouches after they land on the scale. Sanitation ya know. I'm much more precise with the specialty grains. Added the LME and DME and Whirlfloc with the kettle on the burner without any mess or trouble. Loving this LME stuff. The boil dropped off for about 5 minutes while I stirred it in and waited for any possible boil over. Hardly any white foam on top just the narrow Citra hop cones floating in the boil. I think the new finer mesh steeping bag kept more of the fine husk particles out of the wort. Once the amber DME runs out I'll have to go to all LME since its way less messy and about 60% of the price of the dried. Its so cool that I can get it in bulk from the LBS (mainbrew.com). Just hand them your empty container and tell them what and how much you want. The long handled Pyrex measuring cup really makes it easy to get all of the LME in the kettle, just dunk it in until it comes out clean. Probably gonna be a 10 minute cool with the extra wort (10 pints I hope) and only half the Ice on hand (oops) gonna have to use the small cubes and the blue ice packs today. I'll measure the wort in the pan and add cold water to the fermentor to get exactly 10 pints. Luckily the fermentor has a rib line at 10 pints (top side of line) so I can just top up the fermentor from the tap after the pour and strain. The large strainer fits right over the Bubbler's opening so it will be an easier pour with more filter area and a finer mesh so less hops in the fermentor.
