Strong Scotch Liquor Small Batch

an All Grain Strong Scotch Ale by rednender

  • Ingredients
  • 2 Brew Logs
  • 0 Variants
  • 0 Reviews
Print
Export BeerXML

Brewed by rednender

On November 19, 2018

Brewing Notes

My second time brewing this beer. The last time it tasted really cocoa-y/chocolate-y. The problem is, 95% of it spilled into my fermenter due to a loose liquid disconnect swivel barb. I really liked it the first time. The main differences are, I am turning this into a pumpkin beer, I used table sugar instead of Belgian candy sugar, I mashed for 45 minutes instead of 90, I used less water due to the pumpkin, I used an array of base malts, since I had extras of a lot, and I am using S-04 instead of BE-256. Oh, and I didn't mill the cocoa nibs with the grain, but instead added to the boil; this is only because I forgot I had previously milled them. In the future I would mill the nibs. I also used different, but similar hops.

I had an array of left over base malts.

Ingredients:

Water: 3.5 gallons distilled 79 PPM Calcium 5 PPM Magnesium 18 PPM Sodium 50 PPM Sulfate 118 PPM Chloride 48 PPM Bicarbonate

US-S04

Approximates - May update later with actual values 1/3 Marris Otter 1/3 Vienna 1/3 Pale 2-Row Total: 6.3 lb

8 oz Black Malt 3 LBs Roasted Peanut Pumpkin Pumpkin (No flaked barley because of the pumpkin)

.85 lb Table sugar. (I originally measured out a pound of table sugar, but withheld a bit due to the sugar from the pumpkin.) It's worth noting, I used Belgian candy sugar last time.

2 oz Cocoa nibs I did not mill these this time. The last time I made this recipe I milled the cocoa nibs. This time I just added them to the boil instead of the mash. In the future, I would mill them again I think. I just forgot that I had originally milled these

1/2 tsp Pumpkin spice added at first wort 1/2 tsp Cinnamon added at first wort 1/2 tsp Pumpkin spice added after boil 1/2 tsp Cinnamon added after boil

1ozVanguard Hops (I think) 4.5% alpha acid .5oz first wort .5oz post boil

Mash - 45 Minutes Strike Temp : 167F The pumpkin was cold so it dropped the mash temp much lower than I wanted, 144F or so. I hoisted the bag of grain up and heated the liquid for 3 minutes and lowered the bag back down. This stabilized the mash temp at 152F Put in oven at 175F no fan. Mash temp at beginning: 152F Mash Temp at End: 149F Stirred mash every 15 minutes.

The lautering process with the pumpkin is very difficult because the bag doesn't drain very well. Hoisting the bag up and slowly squeezing it to extract as much liquid as possible. Hands get very hot doing this. More insulation during this process would be helpful. Once a sufficient amount of liquid is removed, moved the bag and grain to a colander and bowl setup for further squeezing.

I saved ~3 cups of grain for spent grain cookies. This allowed for further squeezing and more liquid extraction due to the reduced grain amount.

Due to the squeezing and pumpkin, I ended up with a bit more liquid than I anticipated.

Preboil - ~20 minutes Added 1/2 tsp of pumpkin spice and cinnamon spice. I also added ~0.5 oz hops here, half a package. Also added un-milled cocoa nibs at this point. Also added table sugar.

Boil - ~80 minutes. just enough to bring down to around 2.5 gallons estimated. Adder whirfloc with 5 minutes left.

Cooled wort a bit to around 190F before adding second hop addition and pumpkin spice and cinnamon final additions. Continued to chill wort down to 75F, this took about 8 minutes.

Strained and funneled wort into sanitized carboy and pitched yeast.

Setup fermenter with blow off tube just in case
