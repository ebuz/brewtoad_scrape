Brewing Notes

Tasmanian state comp 2014: LAGER version got 4th place in IPA category, bronze medal 101 points. I think it could have done a few points better if my bottle filler (from Corny) was better!

Date Temperature Gravity Notes
08/28/2014 2:41am 2.0 degC 1.016

[37 days] Kegged LAGER after 7.5 days with dry hops (last 1.5 days in fridge). Force carbonated in triple purged keg, also adding gelatin dissolved in 1/2 cup water. Back in fridge now, and should be ready for a sample tonight.
07/25/2014 10:31am 20.0 degC 1.015

[3 days] ALE: pitched at 14ºC and rose to 20°C that evening, but back down to 16°C overnight (even with heat belt). Unfortunately I left the heat belt on that day, and when I returned home it was 26°C!! I removed heat belt and got it down to 20°C where it's been ever since (+/- 2°C). Very surprised even so to see it down to 1.015 already. There's a hint of fusel alcohols in the flavour, but I hope with time it will mellow.
LAGER: it has been very cold. 10.5°C now, but it's been 9-10°C most of the time so far. Smells a bit sulfury but not too bad. SG is only down to 1.048. I will begin to raise temp in ~3 more days once it's around 1.025.
07/22/2014 6:01am 14.0 degC 1.066

[0 days] ALE: pitched 400-500mL thin slurry from Mini Yorkshire (3rd gen); est. 300-350 billion cells (1.25m cells/mL = overpitch). Aerated only by splashing into fermenter - will repeat this in 6 hours. Applied heat belt to raise temp to ~20°C.
LAGER: pitched 2/3 slurry from Ella Pils (2nd gen); est 600-650 billion cells (2.5m cells/mL = overpitch). Aerated only by splashing into fermenter. Put into fridge to get temp down to ~10°C.
08/03/2014 2:38am 12.0 degC 1.011

[12 days] ALE: racked to secondary and dry hopped. Very low FG! Still tastes fusely :-(
LAGER: at 1.031 and 7°C - it's been very cold so I reckon it's been barely moving... Tastes good though. Brought into warmer room (12-15°C), and will apply heat belt tonight.

ALE: bottled yesterday with 65g dextrose for 4g/L CO2.
LAGER: turned on heat belt to finish this one off. Tastes incredible; crystal clear hop flavour, very fruity hops, quite bitter. Going to be great.

Notes

Date Note
07/21/2014 11:15pm

[Lost brewing notes, so the following is my recollection.]
10:12am Mashed in with 23L water at 66°C and hit 59.2°C mash temp.
15min: added 4L boiling water to raise mash temp to 64.6°C; gas OFF.
35min: Mash at ~63°C. Gas to MED and turned on pump to recirculate.
40min: Mash at 64.6°C. Gas to LOW, left pump on.
60min: Mash at ~66°C. Gas to MED, left pump on.
78min: Mash temp at 71.5°C; gas OFF and removed from heat.
90min: Finished vorlauf and began sparge (using pump).
134min: Collected 43L in 44min; wort at 1.057 SG (85% efficiency). Final runnings 1.020 SG and 5.70 pH.
07/21/2014 11:21pm

Adjusted 45L water with 5g CaSO4, 5mL phosphoric acid (85%); added 1.5g CaCO3 directly to mash. Used 27L for mash, then 10L untreated water was added to the remaining 18L for sparge.
Approx water specs should be:
Ca Mg Na SO4 Cl HCO3
52 2 5 64 11 95 (zero after acid addition)
