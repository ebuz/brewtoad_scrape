02/09/2014 5:26pm 70.0 degF —

02/15/2014 8:25pm 69.0 degF 1.021

Removed lid and smelled. Maybe a slight hint of fruit?? Smells like beer but not a milk stout yet. Took gravity reading. First sample wasnt enough to fill thief so i emptied into glass and then took larger sample. Had to spin hydrometer several times to get it to settle right with very few bubbles. Took a few readings and got 1.020. Temp was around 69 so added .001 per hydrometer instructions for a final reading of 1.021. Looks like probably still need to drop an estimated .007 or so.

Transferred to secondary on top of 3oz of Cacao Nibs-Ghana soaked overnight in a baggie with 2oz in weight of Jameson Irish Whiskey. Added nibs/whiskey to sanitized carboy with star san suds still in it. Initially upon primary i thought i had maybe up to 6 gal of wort but when i siphoned to secondary it filled to within a few inches of the top and then had just a little left in bucket (maybe an inch on top of yeast. So looks like I fermented more like 5.5ish gal. Hopefully about right. Added star san to airlock, sanitized around mouth of carboy, airlock, bung plug, etc. Inserted bung/airlock. Had to keep pushing plug back in but hopefully it will all dry and stay seated. Tasted and it seems like its on its way to a milk stout. Mild, a little twinge of something, but I'm hoping that is just the remaining aging/conditioning that needs to happen. At this point I think its still up in the air as to whether this one will work out or not.
02/17/2014 10:38pm 68.0 degF —

No real activity in airlock. Had small amount of activity for maybe 24hrs after transfer to secondary. A bubble every once in a while.

Taste

Poured and drank my first one on 28 Feb. Poured into a porter/stout class cleaned by hand with mild dish soap. Aggressive pour generated a good two fingers of head medium-tan in color. Beer was very dark... Almost black but if you tilted the glass to thin out a view through the beer it was not completely black. Smelled of whiskey due to soaking the nibs. Nice soft mouth feel. Not to thin. Carbonated nicely. Enough to keep a light skin on foam on top as I drank it. Foam was lacy and clung to the glass a little. Very easy to drink. My nose was a little stuffy so it was hard to identify much in the way of smell. Taste was slightly sweet but I'm hoping the chocolate comes out a little more as the bottles sit. Overall I'm very excited about how this beer is turning out. Sort of reminds me of Dragons Milk from New Holland.

Notes

Date Note
02/08/2014 12:00am

Started cleaning at around 1900. Cleaned kettle with 6 tbsp of pbw and sink water filled to brim for 35 mins. Poured some of that to fill the bucket to the brim and included chiller, spoon, paddle, airlock disassembled, thermometer, wire for thermometer mounting, carabiner. Put bucket lid in remaining kettle water to soak. Soaked that for another 35. Dumped water from bucket and rinsed all items. Let air dry on towel. Added 1.5oz of star san to approximately 7 gal of water (filled the furmenter) with chiller, paddle, airlock, lid all inside.

Went to get propane/water. Took between 45-60 mins.

02/08/2014 5:10am

6 Gal of water room temp. Put to flame at 0010hrs.

02/08/2014 5:45am

Reached 160 around 0045. After dropping to between 155 and 160 inserted specialty grains and put lid back on. Closed garage door.

02/08/2014 6:00am

15 minutes into steep temp at 155. Outside temp is 8F. Every 5-10 mins lifted lid and drudged the grains around in the water, checked temp, and returned lid.

02/08/2014 6:15am

At 0115 pulled bag and let drain for several minutes to a tiny trickle. Added malts after soaking tubs in hot water for 30 mins and stirred. Temp at this point was 140.

02/08/2014 6:30am

Returned to heat at 0130 to approach full boil.

02/08/2014 7:05am

Reached hot break and then boil at 0205. Added hops. Hard to keep consistent boil its so cold. Have lid on to help regulate.

02/08/2014 7:55am

Added lactose and hops at 0255.

02/08/2014 8:05am

Removed from heat at 0305 and then inserted cooling coil. Ran cold water for at least 30 mins. Added wort to bucket, brought inside, sanitized yeast, cut with sanitized scissors, pitched, stirred with sanitized paddle, lidded. Before lidding wiped lid with star sans and dripped off excess. Had trouble with air lock. Had to reinstall several times to get it right. Water dripped into bucket. Maybe 10 drops total of bottled water.

02/08/2014 9:15am

Temp at 0415 is 66F. Hope to get a little warmer.
02/15/2014 2:33am

Added 3oz of Cacao Nibs-Ghana to a baggie with 2oz in weight of Jameson Irish Whiskey with the intent to soak overnight to around the 16hr mark. Then, plan to add to secondary and rack on top of it in the 5gal glass carboy for conditioning.
02/24/2014 3:09am

Bottled on the 23rd. Pretty quick/smooth process. Setup some sanitizer (maybe a gallon) in the trashcan lid and put everything in there. Put carboy on the counter and racked into the sanitized bottling bucket. Process for sanitizing big items is typically to put some san in the item, and using rubber gloves, wipe my hands around it inside. Repeat this several times before actually emptying san and racking. Once in bottling bucket, put it on the counter, and connected hoses and bottling wand. Took bottles one by one (or in groups of six) and partially submerged, rolled, and drained the bottles. Sat them aside on the counter, grabbed one by one, and filled. I filled to the top, removed the wand, which provided adequate headspace for 12oz bottles. For larger, I just estimated a few inches or whatever. Capped in groups... so I would san a few, fill a few, and cap a few. Went pretty well. Then used OxyClean Free to clean everything right away.

03/02/2014 9:00pm

Drinking a few now. At the 8 day mark. I really like the whiskey notes, and the other flavors are coming out more too. I think I would maybe make this again without the whiskey next time to make it more palatable for others. But then again, who cares what they think at this point! Its mine... ALL MINE!
