Photos

Beginning of fermentation. Split between two fermenters for extra air contact, plus I've only covered then with sanitised muslin.

Taste

After 3 months in the bottle, this batch is nearly finished, but tasting fantastic. There was definitely a rough flavour up to 1 month ago, probably from the long dry-hopping, but now it's great. Rye spiciness and Ella hops are a very nice combination.

Notes

Date Note
04/13/2014 12:47am

10:31am Mashed in with 12L water at 57°C and hit 53°C mash temp. Added handful of ice cubes to bring mash back to 51°C and held for 20mins. Meanwhile, boiled 6L water for infusion.
10:51 Added 6L boiling water and hit 62.0°C mash temp. Put gas on LOW.
10:58 Mash at 61.7°C; left gas on LOW. Mash pH 5.31 at 50°C, so added 1.5g bicarb to correct.
11:10 Mash at 62.7°C; left gas on LOW. Mash pH 5.39 at 45°C.
11:25 Mash at 64.0°C; turned gas OFF. Mash pH 5.46 at room temp - pH seemed to rise for first half hour of sacc rest (?).
11:47 Mash at 62.0°C; gas to HIGH and infused with 2L boiling water. Stirred constantly.
12:02 Mash at 75°C. Took off gas.
12:16 Finished vorlauf and began sparge. Ran off first 13L in 10mins, then reduced flow rate (to ~0.46L/min) as I started adding sparge water.
1:01pm Finished sparge. Collected 29.5L in 46min (0.64L/min). Boil started almost immediately. Attained 90% efficiency.

04/13/2014 6:28am

Used whole hops which clogged the chiller after half was chilled, so I had to manually filter the other half of the wort and add it back to the chilled wort. Then had to put the whole lot in the fridge to cool down properly. Pitched and aerated the next morning.
