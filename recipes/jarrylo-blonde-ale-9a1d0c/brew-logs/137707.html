Jarrylo Blonde Ale

an All Grain Blonde Ale by Phillip McDaniel

  • Ingredients
  • 1 Brew Logs
  • 1 Variants
  • 0 Reviews
Print
Export BeerXML

Brewed by Phillip McDaniel

On September 10, 2015

Brewing Notes

Mash in with 2.5 gallons for 8 lbs grist @ 1.25 qts per lb. Water at 171.5.

Initial mash temp at 153, added about 24 oz boiling water, raised mash temp to 156.7. At 10 minutes in the mash settled to a perfect mash temp of 154.7.

Hit mashout temp of 169 perfectly, held 15 minutes.

Sparge water = 2.5 gallons at 168-171.

Note: My rule of thumb for mash and sparge water total volume is 2.5 qts water total per each lb of grain. Note you can reduce sparge water or do batch sparge for smaller maltier beers and adjust for lowered efficiency.

Sparge water temp = 170 but fell to 164 2/3rds of the way through sparge, heated back up to 172.

Took about a gallon and a half of vourlaufing to get clear runnings and even then I wasn't totally happy. I think it was because of a more shallow grain-bed vs the IPA from 2 weeks ago.

Boil: Took 40 minutes at 50% power to come to a boil.

Forgot to add Whirlfloc tab! :(

Added hops 14.4 aau Jarrylo at 11-12 minutes left in boil.

Wort chiller works like a bomber, takes 3-4 minutes to get it below 150 (to low actually). Steeping 1 oz Jarrylo for 20 minutes. The Jarrylo hops seemed spicy, black pepper, mildly fruity, herbal in nature more than the specific fruits listed on the package, 'pear, banana, orange, and fruity.'

Chilled for a LONG time. Added 1.4 gallons of 32-35 degree water to top up to 5.4 gallons, temp at 69 degrees exactly.

O.G. - 1.042 on 5.4 gallons of wort. VERY cloudy wort, terrible hot and cold break. Obviously pH and/or chemistry/hardness of water was off.

Final Brew-Day Notes: Placed lid and airlock on fermenter and placed into 32-34 degree fridge to cool to 65 F. When reached I will pitch yeast and brew should take off fairly quickly. Overall it should be a successful brew.

FERMENTATION NOTES: 24 hours after pitching and there are no signs of fermentation including. Originally pitched 50-60 milliliters of quite pure very healthy yeast slurry. Checked the fermenter at 18 hours, zero activity/no co2 aroma, decided to add another 60 milliliters of the same yeast slurry.

09/21/2015 8:24am

09/21/2015, 08:24 AM

Took a sample to see if it was ready to cold-crash in preparation for fining and kegging. It is! It's very simple, straight forward, fairly clean with some fruit tones. Tart lemon orange aroma, good grainy 2-row base with a bit of herbal spice coming through. Toasty bready aroma, really bready when you get past the fruit aromas. Also a bit of yeast bite, beer is quite hazy. Need to hit it hard with gelatin. As I spend a bit of time with the sample I do get a hint of apple-pear aromas, very mild and delicate. Very non-astringent, smooth, mild bitterness isn't focused on rear of tongue, is spread out.

NOTE: I didn't transfer, instead I very gently stirred the chilled beer in the primary and put in 5 grams of prepared gelatin which worked beautifully.
10/05/2015 12:47am

Kegged on 9/26/2015, quick carb method then left on gas at 40 psi for 12-18 hours. Slightly over-carbonated at that point. Raised temp of fridge from 33 to 40. Beer was thin and not shining initially. Now at 8 days after carbonation the beer has settled down. It's serving wonderfully. This is not a full tasting log, just an update.

Appearance is clear, but not quite brite. Pale yellow, very light, less than 5 srm. Thin white lasting head. Moderate lacing.

Aroma/Flavor: nice grainy presence. Simple, not complex. Mildly fruity from hops. Mild citrus and honey.

Finish/Overall. FInishes dry, very drinkable. Enjoyable. Overall not quite as complex as I was shooting for, wanted a bit more hop presence. The Amber malt is nice but sort of not enough. Maybe doing this same grain bill with half pilsner malt might turn out nice. Bittering is balanced, non-astringent, perfectly balanced. The Belgians would say this is very 'digestable'. Can not overstate how mellow this beer is with a lot of goodness and balance. I however want just a bit more hops and a bit more malt complexity.

10/19/2015 3:14pm

A final note. This beer took 1st place in the Light Ales & Lagers category at the 2015 Jacktoberfest homebrew competition in Jackson MS. It then went on to place 3rd in the B.O.S. round which is quite the accomplishment when facing much more flavorful entries. It is a very clean, grainy beer with toast and biscuit notes from the amber malt, mild fruitiness from the hops, very balanced. I still believe a couple pounds of Pilsner malt and a better hop-stand would improve the beer. WLP041 or 039 would both do well with this recipe. Also this recipe went grain to glass in 19 days iirc.

A note on water. Sadly I just started playing with water chemistry using store bought distilled water. As such this brew was the last one I did with my fairly normal mixture of drinking/spring water. Generally I use 8(sometimes a bit over) gallons of water per 5.5 gallon batch. This brew used either 5 or 6 gallons of wal-mart brand 'drinking water' and 2 or 3 gallons of wal-mart brand 'spring water'. Any soft-water profile should do well for the recipe but it needs enough carbonates to compliment the bitterness.
