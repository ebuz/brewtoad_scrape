Brewed by jll23

Brewing Notes

Mashing with about 1.5 gallons. ~.33 of that is distilled water, the rest is campden-treated tap water.

Will "sparge" with about 1.75 gallons of treated tap water.

Definitely going to add a handful of rice hulls to the mash this time.

I first worted a little bit of the hops, just to maybe get a little more bitterness as it's taking forever to boil.

Mash seemingly was decent this time.

Cooled the wort down to about 75 before pitching it on the yeast cake. It really seems like I don't have to be too concerned about pitching temp and such, so I'm not going to be this time. Put the carboy in a closet and half covered it with a blanket. More to keep light out than anything, but hell, if the temperature doesn't matter, then why not?

Finished surprisingly high.

Taste

OG sample is sweet, kind of a dark orange/red color. There is a little bitterness on the finish. Everything seems in order so far.

Tasted a gravity sample that was still pretty sweet. The rye comes through and is pretty nice, but it is slightly overwhelmed by the residual sugars that are currently present. The honey and fruit notes this yeast seems to create are interesting, but they definitely push the overall impression of the beer towards sweet (those are sweet aromas/flavors). It's still too early to tell how this is going to turn out, but I'm not sure how much I love those esters. The thing to do might be to not use many sweet/malty grains in favor of things that taste dryer. That might just create interesting balance in the finished product. We'll see.

At bottling, still light orange. Has all sorts of interesting malt and fruit aromas. Can smell the noble hops as well. I'm still nor really sure how this is going to be after it gets bottle conditioned, but I think it's on the right track. The rye and the sweet malts go pretty well together, the wild card with this yeast are the otherworldly esters. I should note here that the beer did finish quite high. I'm not sure it's very detrimental to the end product, but I have learned to be careful with this yeast while mashing. It doesn't ferment like a Belgian farmhouse yeast might, no matter what its attenuation figures might suggest. In the future, I will just mash in the lower 150's and the let it get as dry as it wants to. The fruity aromas from the yeast will always make it seem sweeter than it is, and that would go double if you use any sweet malts in the beer. Over-attentuation is just not the issue I initially feared it might be. Anyway, I like the rye in this one. That seems like a solid combination with this yeast.

Opening one about a week after bottling, just to see how this thing is progressing. Starting to have some bubbles. It's a really sweet beer at this point. It would be sweet even without any lingering priming sugar. It has a lot of the same sweet fruit/honey aromas that Land Brygge does. Color is about the same, too. I'm not tasting much rye in it right now, which is strange. Maybe the sweetness is just getting in the way. Another odd thing I'm noticing is kind of a punctuating bitterness at the end. No idea where that's coming from. That's about all I've learned so far.

A little under a month in bottle. Surprise gusher. Beer still tastes pretty sweet. I'm not sure if this was just an issue of a bad priming mix or if the high FG is gradually fermenting out. Again, it still tastes sweet, so I doubt it's getting dryer. I get a little bit of rye. Some of the hops (which are nice). A little bit of yeast fruit, but not crazy at this point. It also tastes pretty yeasty because of the gushing. Hoping this was an anomaly, but I'm not sure what to make of this beer based on this one.

Notes

Date Note
05/17/2018 2:26am

It's only been maybe 3 hours since pitching and we're already at vigorous fermentation with a nice top cropping layer of yeast. Temperature is around 76-78F. Crazy turn around. I wonder if it'll just blow through a beer this size in a day or so. I have heard some of these Norwegian sourced strains can do exactly that.

05/17/2018 1:45pm

Airlock is still bubbling steadily, but things look to have slowed down a bit. Krausen has deflated a little. My temp strip only goes up to the high 70's, but this fermentation has clearly entered the 80's. Again, that's not really supposed to matter, but it's always interesting to see if that's truly the case.

05/18/2018 3:28pm

I haven't really been paying close attention to this fermentation, since it seemed less crucial with the yeast. That said, when I checked it today it looks like the krausen has all fallen and the temperature has crashed back into the mid 70's, which is around ambient temperature. As always, I hope that the fermentation is already complete and that the drop in temperature didn't stall the yeast prematurely. With a high temperature fermentation (80+) if was always going to be a big drop once it slowed down, I just hope it got the job done quickly while it was riding those high temps. It still has another week or so to make sure.
