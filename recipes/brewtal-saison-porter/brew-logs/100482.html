Date Temperature Gravity Notes
08/11/2013 3:59am — 1.014

Today I saved a little dog from being run over on my way home from work.

Right now the beer tastes good, I can't wait to see what happens after it has sat for another week or two!

************SPOILERS******************************
There's some bitterness to it but I think that will round out after the secondary fermentation. Some esters. There's a little molasses at the tip of your tongue. I was hoping the gravity would at least be down to 1.012, which it looks like is very possible. The tartness that I've come to expect from saisons and scrumpies is definitely present. I may experiment with this yeast a bit more in a cider or a fruit beer.

Notes

Date Note
08/02/2013 9:36pm

Today I'm testing out the "Brau Beater," it's a Magnetic Stirrer that I've built. The vortex is unbelievably awesome! It uses a wall clock, that I smashed to bits, to send a clock signal to an Arduino. The Arduino snags the signal and writes how many Hours, Minutes and Seconds have elapsed to an LCD. So far so good, it's now at 00:56:09 and the yeast is super happy!
08/02/2013 10:57pm

I'm just about finished with a 30 Minute Steeping of the grain portion of my Wort. The house smells like Chocolate and Bread. While that's going on, I'm doing some yard work. Next, I'm going to get my first Hop addition measured out before I add the DME and Molasses.
08/03/2013 5:05am

I've been running Brau Beater for 4 Hours and 30 Minutes, and I've decided that it's time to pitch the yeast. There's nothing magical about that time, I simply got tired of waiting, and the wort was ready. I'm pitching close to the highest temp that my yeast can function in. The yeast smells spicy and fruity, the wort tastes like caramel and some kind of spicy bitter like if you were to bite into a pickling cucumber. I can't wait to see how the yeast brings it all together.
08/04/2013 3:44am

I woke up at about 7:30 this morning, put on my work clothes, and stumbled into the kitchen for a hot cup of wake me up. Silence was immediately broken by the most disturbing sound. It was like a machine gun that had eaten too many burritos. I looked over at the carboy, about 3 inches of krausen was poking out of the airlock. Like a madman I sanitized a blow-off hose and removed the Airlock, but of course not in that order, my brain left its emergency brake on. After the Star San kicked in, I brushed away a foot of krausen sausage from the top of the carboy and hooked up the hose. The CO2 jetting out was louder and even more expressive now. Lucy, the cat, couldn't take her eyes off the bubbling flask. I never got my coffee. The world works in mysterious ways.
