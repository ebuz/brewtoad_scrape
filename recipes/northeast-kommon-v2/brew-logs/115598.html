04/18/2014 6:22pm

Wyeast package broken (wait 3 hours at least to pitch)
- started sanitizing as well

One-Step Infusion mash:

10:43 - 2 qts of water heating up to about 160
- going to be aiming for a mash temperature of 145-149
1:52 - Mashed in at 159-160 degrees
- target temp of 145-149 stable around the mash
11:02 - temp at 140-142, put on low heat
11:08 - 145-155 temp (avg below 150)
(period of slight decrease in temp to low 40s, put on heat, fast rise to 160 in one section of mash, removed immediately)
11:20 - temp at 144-148
- mash got to low 40s again (this pot doesn't hold heat well), back to heat with constant stirring
11:33 - temps raised to desired levels again
11:36 - some mash reaching temps over 160, stirred, brought down to 145-150 range
Raising temperature at 11:41
Mash out at about 11:43

Recirculate runnings twice
Sparge with 1 gallon of water at 170 degrees

11:53 - heating for hot break

12:04 - Hot break! 60 minute boil started.

Boil 0.2 oz Northern Brewer for 60 minutes

Boil one pinch of Irish Moss for fifteen minutes (12:49)

Boil 2.3 fl oz (4.5 Tbsp) maple syrup for ten minutes (12:54)

Boil 2.3 fl oz (4.5 Tbsp) maple syrup for five minutes (12:59)
Boil 0.2 oz for five minutes (12:59)

Boil the last 0.3 oz for one minute (1:03).

1:30 - temp down to 75-80 degrees.

OG: 1.059

~10 tsp of yeast pitched. (Half the wyeast package)

Blow-off tube on at 1:57
05/02/2014 3:38am

Bottling is becoming more tedious than it should... the slight amount of air that gets through the cane-to-siphon connection is disruptive and causes me to break 2-3 times per siphoning. These breaks worry me because I need to remove the racking cane's endcap and press open the bottle filler to start siphoning again. I fear I've contaminate my brew. We'll see in ten-fourteen days I suppose!

Other than this concern, the color and aroma were great, and the final gravity reading of 1.010 means I've got the 6%+ beer I was hoping for the first time around.

Should note that I did not have the full amount of maple syrup (2.5 tbsp) I needed for priming sugar, had to add 0.5 tbsp of honey.
