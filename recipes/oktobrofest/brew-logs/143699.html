OktoBroFest

an All Grain Oktoberfest/Märzen by Brewce

Brewed by Brewce

On July 13, 2016

Brewing Notes

Directions: Use a two-step mash with a protein rest at 122 degrees F for 25 minutes, followed by a main rest at 152 degrees F for 90 minutes. 1 tsp CaCl and 1 tsp Lactic Acid in Mash. 60 minute boil. Tettnanger hops and Hallertauer Mittelfruh hops added at start of time (boil for 60 minutes). Pitch yeast at 62 degrees F, hold at that temp for 24 hours, then drop to 50 degrees F and hold for 20 days. When fermentation is complete (consider racking to secondary at this point), raise temperature to 65 degrees F for a two-day diacetyl rest, then drop to lagering temp for one month or more. Carbonate.

Brew Day 7/13/2016 Mash: Mash was done in attached garage. Heated 5 gallons of strike water in boil kettle on DarkStar Burner while grains were put into the mash tun. Strike water was then added to mash tun and placed on Edelmetal burner. Protein Rest was at 122 degrees F for ~25 minutes. Main rest was at ~160 degrees F for 60 minutes, plus ~15 minutes with no heat to bring temp down closer to the desired 152 degrees F (Did not do the full 90 minutes due to timing being late in the evening). No CaCl or lactic acid were used in mash, but 1 Tablespoon of PH stabilizer was used. For future, heat mash on the DarkStar burner once the desired temperature is achieved because the Edelmetal burner BTU output was so high that the temperature was difficult to get back down to 152 degrees after it shot up to 160 degrees. The mash tun held temperature surprisingly well even though it was chilly and rainy outside. Sparge: Heated 3 gallons of sparge water in the 6 gallon boil kettle on the Darkstar burner to 170 degrees F. Used glass measuring cup to vorlauf mash tun until wort was clear at the outlet valve, and then gravity fed wort into 10 gallon boil kettle until empty. Added the 3 gallons of sparge water to mash tun and let sit for 15 minutes. Repeated vorlauf until wort was clear. Then gravity fed the mash tun into the boil kettle until pre-boil volume in boil kettle was just over 6 gallons. Boil: Wort was brought to a boil on the Edelmetal burner, and hops were added at the beginning of the boil. Hop weight was estimated since the hop bags were 1 ounce each, we did not have a scale to measure the 0.75 and 0.63 ounce hop quantities. Boil time was 60 minutes. Wort chiller was added to the boil kettle during the last 7 minutes of the boil for sanitizing. After the boil, the wort chiller was hooked up to the hose connection outside the house, and wort temperature was reduced to 70 degrees, which took about 20 minutes. It was raining lightly outside, so I tried my best to keep rain out of the kettle by keeping the lid on, but it is likely small amounts of rain got into the kettle because the chiller prevented the lid from sealing. Yeast: Boil kettle was brought inside and gravity fed from counter top out the kettle valve into a sanitized 6.5 gallon carboy with funnel. Sample was taken to measure the OG. Hose was not used to transfer so that the wort could aerate as it drained into the carboy. In addition, I shook the carboy for 30 seconds to further aerate. The yeast packet and scissors were placed in the sanitizer solution and then I cut open the yeast packet and pitched yeast into the wort. OG was 1.059 and final volume was 5.5 gallons, which equates to ~70% efficiency. Fermentation: I transferred the carboy to the fermentation chamber (freezer), which was temperature controlled to 62 degrees F. This temperature was held for ~18 hours (11 pm on 7/13/16 to 5 pm 7/14/16) and then temperature was dropped to 50 degrees at 5:00 pm on 7/14/2016, which will be held for 20 days. On Thursday July 21, at 3:00 am, the power went out so I was unable to keep the 50 degree temperature until the power was restored on Sunday July 24 at 9:00 pm. During this time the temperature was ~65 degrees, but then dropped to 50 degrees once power was restored. On 8/3/2016 (20 days later), the temperature was raised to 65 degrees F for two days (Diacetyl rest). After the two days, the beer was transferred to a secondary fermentor (5 gallon carboy) on 8/5/2016 and temperature was dropped to ~35 degrees F (lagering temp) which was held for 44 days (desired - one month or more) and bottled on 9/18/2016. Carbonation: Beer was bottled on 9/18/2016. Used approximately 4 oz priming sugar (no scale). FG measured to be 1.022 which results in 4.86% alcohol.
