Elwood the Mountain Boxer Dubbel

an All Grain Belgian Dubbel by Phillip McDaniel

  • Ingredients
  • 1 Brew Logs
  • 0 Variants

Export BeerXML

Brewed by Phillip McDaniel

On January 01, 2016

Brewing Notes

Water Additions: Epsom Salt - 6 (1.3 tsp) Calcium Chloride - 4 (.9 tsp) Baking Soda - 4 (.8 tsp)

Calcium Ca+2 - 50.1 Magnesium Mg+2 - 27.2 Sodium Na - 50.3 Chloride Cl - 88.6 Sulfate SO4-2 - 107.4 Alkalinity (asCaCO3) HCO - 60.272

Mash Report: Mash pH *: 5.36

Mash thickness: 1.26 qt/lb pH Delta from Water: -0.10 effective water residual alkalinity: 45.14 ppm as CaCO3 effective strength of weak acids: 107.22 ppm as CaCO3 * mash prediction is for mash sample cooled to 25 C / 77 F

Overall Water Report: Alkalinity - 66.0 Residual Alkalinity - 14.3

Range Check - All Normal

Range Check SO42-/Cl- ratio: 1.2 Balanced Total lactic acid as equivalent acidulated malt in grist: 1.7 %

Mashed with 3 gals of water for 9.75# grain bill for a mash temp of 151F.

Mashed 60 mins, stirring every 10 minutes.

Raised to mashout temp of 170, took almost 30 minutes to get raised, held @ 170 for 15 minutes.

Vourlaufed for clear wort.

Modified sparge with 2.75 gallons 170F water.

Collected 4.5 or so runnings. Added 2.25 gallons top off water.

Followed recipe for boil with 60/15 min. additions of Styrian Golding hops & 10 min addition of whirlfloc/yeast energizer.

Boiled 60 minutes total.

Chilled to 67 stirred a lot and poured onto 1/2 yeast cake from 'Session Belgian Blonde', wlp 575 abbey yeast blend.

Had a very nice cold-break, & great mash. Chemistry was obviously very good. SUPER clear wort after chilling.

Notes

Date Note
01/02/2016 2:45am

Fermometer reads 70 degrees F. Placed in rubber maid tub with frozen gallon jug. Gotta believe the brew is 2 degrees cooler than that considering it was chilled to 67, but it won't be long before it climbs. Wanting it to stay cool for the first 12-24 hours of fermentation then let free-rise.

01/02/2016 11:47am

Ferm temp at 65. Bubbling steady but not violently.
01/02/2016 10:00pm

Ferm temp at 68F. At midnight I took the fermenter out of rubber maid tube and placed a space heater near it to slowly raise the temp. Trying to raise it about 2 degrees per day till it reaches 74 or till fermentation ends. Fermentation is quite vigorous at this point, more than a bubble per second from airlock.

01/03/2016 8:00am

Temp at 72, probably 71.5. Airlock activity steady as before.

01/03/2016 7:39pm

Temp approaching 74F, probably 73.5. Airlock activity slowed down by approximately 25-30%.
01/22/2016 5:22pm

Kegged. Put on 35 psi for 48 hrs @ 31 F.

01/25/2016 3:22am

Initial Tasting:
Tons of foam at dispensing. Toasty beige/light khaki head, amazing lacing. Muddy hazy very pale brown deep amber hue with more orange/red influence than brown. Nose is estery & phenolic. Malt sweetness comes through along with banana, odd sugar, floral, earthy hibiscus aromas. Belgian spice hits the tongue hard, some pepper, banana, golden raisin, palm sugar notes. Well bittered, nice full mouthfeel that is dancing with phenolics & carbonation. Vienna malt & overall malt character adds complexity and is slightly more than a background note. Overall I've got high hopes. This brew finished a good 5 points higher than anticipated and I had lowered expectations. Currently the sample is murky and unclear, will fine with gelatin if this persists. Overall complex, love the more red than brown color, lacing, phenol/ester balance, mouthfeel. Simply nothing bad to say at this point. Needs another 5 days to settle down and see what it becomes.
03/02/2016 2:57am

Final tasting and comparison to Westmalle Dubbel

The Westmalle Dubbel is 22 months and 21 days after it's bottle date (22 months 21 days old).
Note: All notes are based off of comparison to the Westmalle sample being the standard.

Aroma: 7 of 12. Malt comes through strongly with mild hay, hints of sweetness and light fruity esters. Not nearly as much sweetness esters or phenols on the nose.

Appearance: 3 of 3. Clear/off-clear about 2-4 srm lighter than Westmalle. Thin head lasts slightly less than target but with better lacing.

Flavor: 12 of 20. Nice phenol notes with mild Belgian fruity esters, sweetish but balanced with bitterness. Target is more bready-cookie like. Moderate maltiness with grainy notes. Dry finish that knocks back the desired sweetness. Styrian Golding hops evident with mild spiciness and hint of flora/citrus. Just less all around than target but not bad by any stretch.

Mouthfeel: 4 of 5. Dead on, very good mouthfeel except for being to bitter. Slightly less carbonated than target, only truly noticable/impacting early on when still very cold. Drinks very well, perceived as too dry thanks to elevated bitter finish.

Overall: 7 of 10. Absolutely nothing wrong with the beer, drinks wonderfully and is a fair-middling dubbel. Compared to the best example in the world it falls short. Can not say enough that it is a pleasure to drink, VERY clean. I think the short comings are from yeast selection being the blend rather than normally using wlp530 the actual Westmalle blend and my favorite Belgian/Trappist yeast. More importantly this was brewed in winter and stayed to low for to long with little rise of ferment temps at the end. I'll enjoy this beer, am proud of it. It simply falls short of being an ideal example of the style.

Notes for next attempt: No more experimental sugar, use the proper Belgian candy sugar or if being cheap plain table sugar or brown sugar. Bitter by 25% less and look at water profile to reduce bitter impact/increase maltyness (VERY important to reduce sulfates). Use wlp530, other single strain in a pinch.
