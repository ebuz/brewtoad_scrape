an All Grain Munich Dunkel by Phillip McDaniel

Brewed by Phillip McDaniel

On January 24, 2016

Brewing Notes

Hit all numbers but had a much higher efficiency at 88% vs normal 80-81%. This has occurred on 2 of last 3 brews since I've begun water treatments. Boiled for 100 minutes due to a slow initial boil thanks to sub-freezing temps. Only potential problem at all from the brew day is the possibility of over-sparging by a half gallon or so.

Chilled to 56.5, pitched decanted yeast at approximately 55-56 degrees. First 12 hours after pitching, temps are holding at 56 F. Opened window with temps in the 50s during the day.

Temp at 54 F. A bubble every 5 seconds.
01/29/2016 10:39am

My fermentation temps are all over the place, stressing the yeast in the process I'm sure. I need smaller water bottles as my 1 gal jugs drop the temp by as much as 8 degrees. I was gone yesterday after not adding a bottle in the morning and returned close to midnight to find the temp at 59 F. I put about 20 pieces of ice and a frozen 1 gallon jug in the swamp cooler. This morning my temp was 50 F with slowed activity. That may not be a bad thing as it is still producing a bubble ever 4.6 seconds.

Moved to keg. Let sit at room temps of low to mid 60s since last note, several weeks. It took almost a week for fermentation to finish at 60F with very very slow activity. Final Gravity of 1.017 measured at racking. Is sitting in a 30-32F fridge till serving.

Tasting notes: Nose is pilsner grassy malt character with a hint of char and fruit. Flavor is FAR too fruity meaning the late extra long diacetyl rest was too much and added fruity esters. Rather disappointed at this point. It is otherwise clean with a mild lager character and clean crisp finish.
