an All Grain American IPA by Brumanchu

Brewed by Brumanchu

On May 25, 2013

Brewing Notes

Wow, not the smoothest strart to my brew day. I decided I would mash with 6 gallons of water in my 7.5 gallon pot so I brought it up to temp(161) and started to add the 13 lbs of grain for this recipe....wasn't going to fit, I've gotten lazy and didn't even check to make sure it would beforehand so I learned my lesson. Had to remove some water and put us it in my sparge. Mash temps started out near perfect at 151, then rose to 153, added ice, rose to 154, added more ice, dropped to 145, added heat, took forever but got back to 151 and the about 30 minutes in dropped to 148 where it stayed the rest of the mash. I've got to get my mash temps under control, or myself since I don't wait as long as I should to make sure the temp is stable. I ended up with more wort than would comfortably fit in my brewpot so I had another small pot going for the first 25 minutes of the boil and then combined before my 60 minute hop addition(90 min boil). Preboil gravity was 1.054 so estimate 1.69 for 5.25 gallons at the end of the boil. I may add some water to keep the gravity near the recipe specs of 1.064. I don't want it to be too dry might and end up missing my 1.010 target for F.G. since this should be somewhat dry for an IPA. The wort is a beautiful reddish gold and looks fantastic. Had a very good hot break and can tell this will be clear after fermentation. Pitched with 700ml of slurry of BRY-097 west coast ale yeast and activity is pretty strong 5 hours after pitching(4pm-9pm) O.G. on this beer is 1.064 to 1.066 and ended with 5.5 gallons or slightly more in the fermenter. I was shooting for 1.064 at 5 gallons so my efficiency must have been pretty good. I think this will be a very nice beer, looks good, smells good, tastes good going into the better bottle. One week after brewing 06/01/13 took a sample and the gravity reading right now is 1.006, lower than the 1.010 that I had hoped for. I'm stoked on the BRY-097 right now, I started with a blow-off tube and I'm glad I did, it fermented very vigorously and was pushing krausen out of the blow-off in 15 hours or so. this should come out to be about nearly 8% after the priming sugar addition. The color is going to be beautiful and spot on for an IPA.
