Brewing Notes

To Celebrate National Hombrewing day, the American Homebrewers Association organizes clubs and members to host BIG BREW Days all over. The Rock Hopper Brew Club gathered at C.B. & Potts in Highlands Ranch Colorado to brew. I brewed the 3rd version of an IPA I have been working on for the last year.

The Brewery had water with a temperature of 160-165°F for us to use. The Ph was also quite a bit higher than what I'm used to. I choose to use this water to prewarm my CoolerTun, as well as mash with. The temperature once the H2O had drained from the Tun had reached my striking temperature of 159°F. When I doughed in, my mash temperature came out to 155°F, and by the end of the mash was 152°F. I was shooting for 148°F. My 1st runnings his about 1.032 after the Vorlauf and lauter of 5.55 gallons. It may have been closer to 6 gallons before It mostly ran clear. This was the first time experimenting with using a grain bag intended for BIAB. It made for an easy cleanup, but my grain bed never quite set right. I achieved a 1.060 during my 2nd runnings, and my combined runnings came out to 1.056.

I ran a smooth 60 minute Boil, and ended with a 1.069 Initial Gravity, which is only 4 points greater than the Recipe using 6 Row. I hooked up the Plate Chiller, and the wort collected was at about 60°F. I put everything into the Car, and had a Beer at C.B. & Potts. When I got the wort home, the temperature had risen to 80°F, so I waited to pitch the yeast.

The Yeast was pitched nearly 24 Hours later.
