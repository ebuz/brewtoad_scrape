Racked onto fruit in secondary last Wed. Tonight I removed the lid from secondary (I needed it for a batch I brewed tonight) and was smacked in the face with a damp beery strawberry shortcake smell. It was awesome. I'll check final gravity in another 5 days but fermentation was quite active with a significant krausen I normally only see in primary. Stoked.
06/12/2014 11:00pm — 1.022

Fermentation is still going on after 10 days, still a "steady bubble" every 15-30 seconds. Took a gravity and its got a way to go. Also I took a sip of the wort can taste the alcohol in the beer even at current gravity of 1.022 and that's because I think the OG was about 1.061 not 1.052, so there's going to be a lot more punch to this batch. Oops!

Note to self, back off on some of the sorghum to bring the gravity down 10 points.
06/23/2014 1:36am 66.0 degF 1.016

I think this thing is done. 1.016 SG now. There's a mat of strawberries floating on top and the beer is quite cloudy and pink in appearance. It has a pleasant sourness (sorghum twang, green beer) and is a bit "boozy". It has a forward strawberry aroma and taste but is not overpowering. Tastes like flat but sour Tweasonale, but much thicker and pinker from the strawberry mash.

This has a potentional to be a winner. At 5.9 % ABV its probably not a summer "session" beer but I just checked and DFH Tweason Ale is 6% ABV so we're right "on recipe" here.

Taste

Taste Rating
38 / 50

On day 11 in primary fermenter, I took a sip of the wort. I could already taste the alcohol in the beer and its only at 1.022 currently. This is because I mistyped the honey fermentable and this my target OG was 1.052 but actual was probably 1.062. This puts the beer at around 5% abv already and that doesn't include a secondary fermentation that appears almost guaranteed when I rack onto fruit in secondary.

On day 5 of force carbonation, I poured a small 3-4 ounce sample and had a sip. Carbonation is nearly complete. Color is a light pink and cloudy with bubbles present on the side of the glass. Small pale head and a strawberry nose. Taste starts with a big strawberry nose and finishes dry, almost like a dry champagne with a sour note likely caused by the sorghum. I find the sour note pleasant and not quite the sorghum twang I've tasted before.

After 7 days (June 28th), the beer is fully carbed and tastes very good. Its July 1 and the beer is pouring great and tastes nice. I think the taste will hit its peak and remain there in 3-5 days and be great until August. By then it might mellow or not... but it might also be gone. Still trying to figure out how these sorghum based beers mellow after 2, 4 and 8 weeks. A "Grapefruit IPA" that is in keg for 3 months and almost gone is absolutely fantastic right now.

Notes

Date Note
05/31/2014 10:00pm

This was done at a multiple batch brew day at the Anderson's.

Batch was 5.5 gals to start using RO filtered water.

One new thing I did was to add hot tap water into my old kettle with the honey, sorghum and candi syrup in the kettle to soften it. I added water up to the cap line so no water leaks into the extracts. This made pouring extracts and scooping out correct amounts of honey much easier. I used a kitchen scale this time to measure out 2 lbs/ 908g of RUBW honey.

Boil was uneventful. Added extracts and hops on schedule according to recipe.

I added about 2 (liquid) oz of leftover Belgian Candi D-45 from a previous batch. Figured it couldn't hurt but I may remove it from the recipe because its completely unnecessary.

I moved the chilled wort into the primary by using the valve and letting it splash liberally into the bucket. I then poured all the contents of the kettle, including any solids, into the primary. (There was very few solids this time, compared to the GF IPA I did last time, undoubtedly due o the low hop schedule, so the sorghum and honey themselves don't impart too much more solids than a typical barley based extract recipe-its the hop pellets most likely.)

I forgot to take a gravity reading so the reading I entered was ESTIMATED based on brewtoad's estimates here.

its day 5 of force carbonation, I poured a small 3-4 ounce sample and had a sip. Carbonation is nearly complete. Color is a light pink and cloudy with bubbles present on the side of the glass. Small pale head and a strawberry nose. Taste starts with a big strawberry nose and finishes dry, almost like a dry champagne with a sour note likely caused by the sorghum. I find the sour note pleasant and not quite the sorghum twang I've tasted before.

This recipe is a definitive success in my opinion. I like it better than DFH Tweasonale due to the forefront punch of strawberry and the drier finish and pleasant sour aftertaste. The mouthfeel is light like DFH's but it feels a little less thin and interesting than dfh's.

My wife feels this is a delicious beer. She also loves the DFH Tweasonale and tended to like German wheat beers and Hooegaarden (back before she went GF).

I believe this recipe could be re-brewed using raspberries or huckleberries with similar success and plan to repeat the batch using what is fresh and in season.
