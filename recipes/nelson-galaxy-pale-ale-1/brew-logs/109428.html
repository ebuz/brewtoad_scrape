Nelson Galaxy Pale Ale

an All Grain American Pale Ale by Nate

  • Ingredients
  • 3 Brew Logs

  • 0 Reviews
Print
Export BeerXML

Brewed by Nate

On January 01, 2014

Brewing Notes

Due to the thin body, 6oz of maltodextrin were added to the keg (post carbonation) to try to correct the FG and add some body back to it. The powder was boiled for 5 minutes in a cup of water to dissolve and sterilize. The pressure was let off the keg and the keg was placed in a bath tub (in case it turned into a volcano). The hot liquid was poured into the keg and a little foaming occurred but not bad. The pressure was put back on the keg and the head space was blown out a few times to get rid of the oxygen.

Taste

Force carbonated a bottle after kegging to give it a try. The flavor is certainly tropical fruit, but I'm not sure it is what I was expecting. Bitterness is pretty high, but that will drop some with a week or two in the keg. Flavor is a bit green, so a week or two is probably needed before really pouring it on a regular basis. Nice and dry, good body. Aroma is a bit more like bruised fruit than fresh cut fruit, maybe that is the galaxy hops?

After kegging and letting a little carbonation get into the beer, the mouthfeel is very thin. This will probably improve some with the carbonation level, but the recipe could use something to thicken it up a bit ( flaked oats/barley, crystal 20, carapils, etc). Bitterness is still strong, but this is the first couple draws from the keg which tend to be more bitter as the yeast and stuff settle out.

After full carbonation, the body is ok but very light. This needs something to build more body into it. That said, it is very drinkable and a pint goes down quick. Flavor is good, just a body problem. The finish is very dry , which is nice and should be maintained if the body is increased.

To try to correct the body, 6oz of maltodextrin boiled in 1 cup of water was added to the keg to bring the FG up to the original desired level of 1.010. After trying the change, the body is fine now. The bitterness is still pretty high and the beer is pretty cloudy. May try fining with gelatin in the keg to clear it up and strip some of the bitterness out.

3 Weeks on, the beer has totally cleared and the flavor is great. 5 out of 5 coworks agree, it is yummy and very drinkable.
