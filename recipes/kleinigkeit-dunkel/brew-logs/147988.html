an All Grain Munich Dunkel by jll23

Brewed by jll23

On February 15, 2017

Brewing Notes

Mashing with about 1.75 gallons. Sparge with about 1.5. Will top up with cold water at the end. I need to cool the wort down to about 55F (target fermentation temperature) anyway. Distilled water was added to mash water to dilute natural minerals in my tap water (which was also treated with campden for chlorine).

Fermentation space seems to be around 56F. Unfortunately for me, we're supposed to hit the 60's in a few days. I'm currently unsure of what this might do to the fermentation space's temperature. I was originally angling for a yeast that could go up into the low 60's (just in case), but I'll have to settle for the possibility of fermenting this one a little warm. The last one I did that way seemed to turn out fine.

Cooled wort to about 55F before pitching yeast and placing in the basement. Mash didn't suck again this time (though again I was going for a smaller OG).

Our seemingly annual February heat wave brought outside temperatures into the low 70's, but my fermentation space thankfully remained significantly cooler. 2/18 seemed to be the hottest temperature for the actual beer itself, at right around 60F. By 2/19, the temperature had dipped back around 58F, though fermentation is still active. All in all, that's not too bad, considering. The beer does seem to be throwing off a fair amount of sulfur right now, but with temperatures hitting 60F, I shouldn't have to worry much about diacetyl (though I will likely bring the carboy upstairs for a few days into mid-60F temperatures for a few days before I rack anyway).

Hit it with a decent dose of priming sugar at bottling. I lost a lot of beer along the way, but I'm erring on the side of more carbonation than less. Also got a pack of Safale US-05 and added a little of that to hopefully speed up the carbonation process (learned my lesson from the last lager I made).

Taste

At bottling, it's a very dark beer with lots of good Munich malt taste and even some chocolatey notes. I may have overdone it with the carafa, but the result seems tasty so far.

I started drinking this beer around 3 weeks in the bottle and some interesting things were happening around that time. First, and for the first couple I tasted, the yeast refermenting was creating all sorts of interesting fruity flavors. And these were really specific fruity flavors. It basically tasted like a sweet, fruity red wine in my beer glass, with a few dark German malt flavors thrown in for interest. It was weird, but kind of alluring. Within a few days of that phenomenon, the beer just kind of snapped into focus, big time. Suddenly, the Munich malts that made up most of the beer just came on with a vengeance and the lager yeast really receded into a perfectly elegant presentation context for amazingly Bavarian malt flavors. Dark, bready notes with hints of chocolate. Sometimes there are even pseudo-roasty notes from the carafa. More than anything though, it was just amazing how close to the traditional dopplebock flavors this got. Some dunkels are definitely in that vein and I was beyond pleased to hit these amazing tastes right on the head. I have noticed a lot of variance between bottles, however. Some are more roasty, some have more muddled malt character. All in all, really impressed with how this turned out, especially since it was a big question mark at bottling. Easily demonstrates the worthiness of lager beer.
