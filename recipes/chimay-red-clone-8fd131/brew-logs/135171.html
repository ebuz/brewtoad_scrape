Brewing Notes

[note, based on this mash extraction, mash efficiency was around 80-85%]

Wanted to use Chimay bottle culture, but it didn't seem to be active after 2 or 3 days in a starter. Bought Wyeast 1214 Belgian Abbey Ale (supposed to be 'same' as Chimay), on brew day and mixed that with the attempted bottle harvest starter (which seemed to be inactive even after 3 days). Pitch the whole mix, we'll see how fermentation goes..

Taste

initial impressions (will come back to this later, like ~1 month after bottling):
A - slightly hazy copper honey (smooth, opacity is in the beer not due to pouring yeast). Almost no head, and no lacing
S - malty, a little fruity, alcohol
T - initially sweet, sweet and slightly dark bitter dry fruitiness and yeast esters, slightly phenolic. finishes somewhat bitter
M - a little chewy, but alcohol dryness is prominent. not strongly carbonated, so body seems maybe thicker than it might be otherwise
O - taste seems well balanced, but not sure if the alcohol is a little harsh. wait and see if this mellows out with longer time in the bottle

will want to let it wait a little more, and eventually compare side-by-side with chimay red and other belgian dubbels
worry that it may have gotten too 'harsh' in the alcohol and others from fermenting

BJCP Judges at Eli's comp (July 2015, I think it has improved noticeably in the 2+ months since then) placed 6 out of 14 in Belgian category. Two judges scored 30 and 31 out of 50. Both noted it would have been better with a bit more malt complexity (add some Special B or others), and should be better balanced to have less candi sugar character, and might be improved by even higher attenuation.
