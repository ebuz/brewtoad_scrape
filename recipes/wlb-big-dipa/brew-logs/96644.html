WLB - Big DIPA

an All Grain American IPA by White Lodge Beer

Brewed by White Lodge Beer

On May 27, 2013

Brewing Notes

Dogfish Head 60 minute IPA clone

Style: American IPA Yield: 17 L (4.5 US gallons) Original Gravity: 1.066 Final Gravity: 1.015-1.016 IBU: 60 SRM: 9 ABV: 6.1%

All-grain Method:

Mash the following malt below for 90 minutes at 67 °C :

  • 5.9 kg Maris Otter pale malt (British 2-row pale malt)

  • 0.113 kg British Amber malt

Mash input: +18.5L water for mash @ 3.1L / Kg (@75 °C before adding grain - cooling down to 66 °C after adding grain. 90 minute mash) -7.5L water lost to grain absorption & mash tun dead space 1st runnings output: +11L

Batch sparge input: +13L water for batch sparge @ 2.16L / Kg @ 75 °C -1L water lost 2nd runnings output: +12L

Total Post Mash & Batch sparge volume = 23L @ SG 1.065

+3L top-up water after batch sparge = Total Pre-boil volume = 26L @ SG 1.0575 (Estimate, not measured).

Step 1. Take the Safale US-05 11.5g dry yeast out of the fridge 3-5 hours before the end of the chilling phase.

Step 2. Clean and then sanitize the following equipment:

  • Brewpot (Clean)

  • Mash paddle (Clean)

  • Stirring spoon (Clean)

  • Yeast packet (Clean / Sanitize)

  • Fermenter and Lid (Clean / Sanitize)

  • Airlock (Clean / Sanitize)

  • Thermometer (Clean / Sanitize)

Step 3. Heat 25L (18.5L for the Mash and the rest for heating the tun etc) of water to 80°C.

Step 4. Measure out the 6kg grain and take the ambient temperature (Actual was 20°C).

Step 5. Preheat the Mash tun with some boiling water (3-5L), swirl it around to heat the tun up, replace water back to boiler.

Step 6. Begin the Mashing: Add 18.5L @ 80°C to the Mash-tun, allow to drop to @75 °C.

Step 7. Stir the grist into the Mash-tun and once all the grain is in (fully wetted) take the temperature to ensure it has stabilized at the target temperature of 67 °C or in between 66-68°C (the Thermos mash-tun, with sleeping bag insulation, only loses 1°C over 90 minutes). Place the thermometer into mash, close lid and wrap sleeping bag around.

Step 8. Meanwhile heat the 13L of sparge water (having topped up the boiler to 20L to 80°C but no higher).

Step 9. After 90 minutes of mashing. Recirculate. Open the valve slowly and drain about 2L of the strike water and gently pour back into the grain bed recirculating the wort. Repeat until the wort exiting the mash tun is pretty clear.

Step 10. 1st runnings. Once the wort is clear drain it carefully to the cleaned fermenter (open the valve slowly and drain slowly - don’t drain too quickly as this may compact the grain bed). (Actual 1st runnings volume 11L).

Step 11. Once the 13L of sparge water is at 75-76°C perform the batch sparge. Close the valve and add the 13L of sparge water. Stir grist thoroughly to dissolve as much remaining sugar into the wort as possible. There is a chance of dissolving unconverted starch into the wort so let the mash sit for 15 mins to allow residual alpha-amylase to convert to sugars.

Step 12. 2nd runnings. Recirculate and drain to the fermenter with the 1st runnings.

Step 13. Measure the gravity. Target is 1.064 Original Gravity. (Actual total volume, 1st + 2nd runnings, was 23L @ SG 1.065)

Step 14. Add 3L to the 1st and 2nd runnings in the fermenter and then transfer to the boiler (Actual Pre-boil volume = 26L @ SG 1.0575 (Estimate, not measured))

Step 15. Total Boil duration: 60 minutes

Hop additions:

At the beginning of the Boil (0 mins) add:

  • 15.4g Warrior leaf (bittering hop)

After 30 mins add:

  • 7.7g Warrior leaf (bittering/flavour hop)

  • 7.7g Simcoe leaf (bittering/flavour hop)

  • 7.7g Amarillo leaf (bittering/flavour hop)

After 45 mins add:

  • 7g Warrior leaf (flavour hop)

  • 7g Simcoe leaf (flavour hop)

  • 7g Amarillo leaf (flavour hop)

  • 1 tsp (5ml) Irish moss (Rehydrate it in warm water before use for best results).

Add the immersion chiller to the boil to sterilise the chiller.

After 50 mins add:

  • 7g Warrior leaf (flavour/aroma hop)

  • 7g Simcoe leaf (flavour/aroma hop)

  • 7g Amarillo leaf (flavour/aroma hop)

After 55 mins add:

  • 7g Warrior leaf (aroma hop)

  • 7g Simcoe leaf (aroma hop)

  • 7g Amarillo leaf (aroma hop)

After 59 mins add:

  • 7g Warrior leaf (aroma hop)

  • 7g Simcoe leaf (aroma hop)

  • 7g Amarillo leaf (aroma hop)

After 60 mins: Stop heating. Measure the Post-boil/Pre-Fermenter fluid volume. (Actual Post-Boil volume = 23L after 60 minute boil = 11.5% loss of pre-boil volume = 3L)

Step 16. Begin chilling the wort using the immersion chiller to a target temperature of below 21°C. SANITISE THE THERMOMETER WHEN CHECKING THE TEMPERATURE.

Step 17. Whilst chilling the wort clean the fermenter and then fully sanitize the fermenter bucket, lid and airlock.

Step 18. Once below 21°C move the boiler to the edge of the worktop, then sanitize the worktop, to ensure that as the pour rate decreases there is no possibility of any cooled wort splashing of the worktop and into the fermenter.

Step 19. Place the fermenter on a chair and open up the boiler tap and let the cooled wort drop into the fermenter creating lots of foam in the process to aerate the wort.

Step 20. Towards the end of the transfer take a sample for a specific gravity reading and cool to 20°C for hydrometer (sanitize the jar before placing into the wort stream).

Step 21. Once wort transfer over measure the volume of wort in the fermenter, sanitize the lid one more time, then sprinkle the dry yeast over the foam and close the lid of the fermented. (Actual final volume in fermenter = 17L @ OG 1.066, losing 6L to hops and trub in the boiler - in hindsight probably could have drained more wort).

Step 22. Ferment in the primary fermenter and after 7 days take a hydrometer sample using a fully santized Thief. Then 2/3 days later take another sample. If the gravity is the same (stable) fermentation is done.

Step 23. Then add the dry-hop additions to the fermenter (in a boiled/santized muslin bag):

  • 7g Warrior leaf (dry hop)

  • 7g Simcoe leaf (dry hop)

  • 7g Amarillo leaf (dry hop)

Ste 24. Leave for another 3-5 days then bottle and leave for a minimum of two weeks.
