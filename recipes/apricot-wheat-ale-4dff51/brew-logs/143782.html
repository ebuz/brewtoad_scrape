Apricot Wheat Ale

an All Grain Fruit Beer by Mark Stone

Export BeerXML

Brewed by Mark Stone

On June 21, 2016

Brewing Notes

Tuesday brew day.

  • Mash temp settled at exactly 154°F.
  • pH settled at 5.13 pH
  • First runnings: 1.068 S.G.
  • Combined runnings: 6.0 gal / 1.038 S.G.
  • Topped off to 6.6 gal and stirred. 1.040 S.G. (Prior reading was taken before stirring.)
  • 1.044 O.G.

Experienced my first stuck sparge. Tried everything to free it to no avail. Ended up having to dump the entire mash into a bucket, clean out the false bottom and valve, then transfer the mash back to the tun. I hope I didn't create any oxidation issues with all the hot-side aeration. Note to self: lauter extra slowly when using wheat malt. (Even with rice hulls.)

Overall, really happy to hit my pH target almost right on. The water / pH calculator at Brewer's Friend is really impressive. This was the first brew I have considered mash pH for. Glad I did, it would have been way off without the acidulated malt. I did end up a bit short on volume once again, and topped off with tap water. I ended up with more wort than usual in the fementer. I guess it will take time to nail down exact volumes.

(Edit 08-22-2016: Removing the acidulated malt would have put me at 5.4 pH, which is arguably "better" than 5.13. I was a little confused when first working out pH levels...)

--

The following is taken from https://www.homebrewersassociation.org/forum/index.php?topic=14570.0

If [apricot puree] is 1.040 diluted to 1 gallon, then [...]

40pts * ((16 cups/gallon) / 5 cups) = 128 gravity points

So 5 cups of undiluted purree would have a gravity of 1.128.

So:

40pts * (1 gal / 5.5 gal) = 7.27pts

So [...] you can assume that the purree will raise the OG of your beer by 0.007.

Taste

Taste Rating
44 / 50

Soooooo, this turned out pretty damn good. Of all the beers I've made, I am pleased to be able to say I don't detect the slightest hint of off flavor. It is also the first beer I make that does not contain any flavoring hops. (Maybe I'm onto something here.) This beer is wonderfully clean tasting, with a nice sour touch. I was worried it would be too dry, but it is perfectly balanced when served cold. I served this at Tamara's girls' night yesterday and got unanimous 5-star ratings, it was a big hit. Edit: A few weeks later, both Tamara and I agree that the beer could be a little sweeter. Consider adding more crystal.
