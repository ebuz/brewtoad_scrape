Bambina

an All Grain Traditional Bock by Jake Wyse

Brewed by Jake Wyse

On January 08, 2016

Brewing Notes

7 gal strike water heated to 170 Pot placed in insulation wrap Waited until top read 166 Mashed in (too hastily) Initial mash temp 158-159 157-158 after 15 minutes pH 5.4 (dark beers 4ever)

After 30 min (152 F), 2 gal liquid decocted from spout Decoction boiled for 30 min (lost ~0.5 gal) Added back to main mash (156 F)

Bag lifted from mash and drained Bag placed into 2 gal water at 180 F Batch sparge mixed Hops added to first runnings and placed on heat Bag lifted from sparge and drained 7.25 gal sweet wort at 1.056 (85% efficiency!)

Boiled for 60 min Whirlpooled and cooled with counterflow chiller 5 gal wort at 1.068

5 L yeast starter won't flocculate Wort and yeast left in cooler at 35

Yeast looks like it's fermented out. Set fermentation chamber to 50 F and put it on ice; looked like it was settling a bit after a few hours. Yeast left overnight in the fermentation chamber.

Big tan krausen. Lots of activity in the blowoff tube. No wort in the blowoff tube yet.
01/19/2016 3:45am 50.0 degF 1.030

Krausen dying down and >50% attenuated. Starting 36-hour ramp to 65 F for diacetyl rest.
01/21/2016 7:00am 65.0 degF 1.028

Fermentation looks like it's dying down, and still 1.028. Taste, however, is good; not too sweet, despite high gravity, and nice malty flavor with strong melanoidins. Still perceptible diacetyl.
02/01/2016 3:00am 50.0 degF 1.028

Temp controller set at 30 and freezer running, but for some reason beer is at 50 and was this morning too. First check since back from Guatemala. Very tasty. Very oaky but not overwhelming. Hopefully it will get (back?) down to lager temp and won't pick up any more oak. Many oak chips are still floating.
02/03/2016 5:00am 52.0 degF —

Fridge still not getting cold. Put carboy outside, tried to investigate wiring (tested voltages) in fridge to no avail. Put fridge outside w/ beer inside under a tarp. Added ice for warm days, added water and scooped out ice to prevent freezing on cold nights. Lagered for 8-9 days before bottling.
