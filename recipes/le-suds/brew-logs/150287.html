Brewed by jll23

On July 15, 2017

Brewing Notes

Mashed with 2 gallons of water. This was made of a little over .5 gallon distilled water and the rest campden treated tap water.

Sparged with about 1.5 gallons campden treated tap water.

Cooled the wort to about 72F and then pitched it on the old yeast cake. With this added volume, there's not much headspace in my 3 gallon carboy. Based on what I saw with the last fermentation, that shouldn't really be a problem.

Tossed in a few extra pellet pieces while the wort was cooling.

Taste

I gave this beer a lot of time before bottling, partly because I expected it would be pretty strong (and therefore more aging wouldn't hurt), and partly due to sheer laziness. The beer sat in "transfering position" for over a week and it was still super cloudy when I bottled it. I even cranked the A/C to try and crash some of yeast. No such luck. The FG is extremely low, almost everything fermented out, so I do wonder what all the yeast left in suspension was waiting for. Oh, well.

At bottling, this beer is a cloudy light orange color. As intended, you can really smell and taste the hops on this one. It's that distinct currant flavor mixed with other almost tropical fruity flavors. The beer is objectively dry, but it really doesn't taste that way. There's a creaminess that is suggested both by the hop fruit flavors and from this great yeast strain. It's a beast, and will finish low, but you wouldn't know it from the beers it produces. I do often worry about a beer getting too bitter if the bottom falls out of it, but this one really doesn't seem to have that problem. The Vienna adds a really nice malt impression that helps balance some things out. This is a strong beer, but in keeping with the theme, it's doing a good job of hiding that extreme as well. So far I'm liking it. It's a little fruitier/hoppier, with less malt complexity than the beer I made before this (with the same yeast and hops). Seems like what I was going for.

Trying this a little over a week after bottling. Malty, but on the lighter side, thanks to the vienna. Bitterness is more pronounced than the last one. So is the hop aroma/taste. There's a little more intensity in terms of strength/booze, but still a pretty mild-drinking beer. Just a more hop forward/less malt focused beer. Currant/fruity/sweet orange in the hops. Malt aroma is kind of bready/sweet. Again amazing given how low the FG is.

Trying this again after about 20 days in the bottle. Definitely taste the vienna. Sweet yet spicy, as is its way. This is the first beer I've had in over a week, so I can definitely taste the booze on this one a bit more (though the spice is both from the vienna and the booze, I think). Hop aroma is fruity, but sort of subdued. I'm not sure, but I think this beer might actually benefit from some further aging. It won't help the hops, but it might mellow the rest of it in a pleasant way.

Been about 6 weeks in bottle. It's pleasant at this point in the night. One thing I want to focus on is the funk I taste tonight. Honest to goodness barnyard beyond all the fruity currant hops and spiciness (from the Vienna, yeast, and maybe booze). It's interesting because I just tasted my brett saison earlier and that gamey flavor is starting to morph (how I'm not sure yet) after a few months in addition to what is primarily a tropical brett flavoring. I had attributed that to wild yeast, and maybe it is. There's a part of me that wonders if it's not part of the saison/farmhouse base (which would explain why I find it in this one too). Or, it could just be I've somehow become infected again. That would suck. The point remains that there is some deep seated funk in this beer. That is (as previously mused) not unheard of in this kind of yeast, though I definitely find the Biere de Garde yeast a lot cleaner than your typical saisons. Ultimately, this beer is both strong and dry, a dangerous combination if you're looking for subtlety. It navigates that gap fairly well, but there are times when I wish it were somehow subtler. But, that's like wishing the frankenstein's monster you created were somehow more flame resistant. It is what it is, and damn, what an achievement.
