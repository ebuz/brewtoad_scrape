Bam Biere clone

an All Grain Belgian Specialty Ale by Rollermt

Brewed by Rollermt

On June 26, 2017

Brewing Notes

Supplies: - HBS did not have Crystal hops. Purchased Mt. Hood (5.6 AAU) (for bittering) and German Hallertau (3.8 AAU) instead. - Used WLP500 instead of Wyeast equivalent - No starter since starting gravity is fairly low - Used a pinch of roaster barley (approx. 4-8 grains) to mimic "rustic" story of Bam Biere concept (via CYBI podcast).

Mash: - Brought 5 gal of water up, target water temp 165. Target mash = 149 - 9.75 pounds of grain. Water to grist ratio: ~2qt/1# (thin mash) - Mash in, hit 160. Covered with foil and blanket to hold temp. At 15 min. temp = 151. At 35 min. temp = 149. At 50 min. temp = 147. - Added 10 min. to mash as sparge water cooled a bit.

Sparge: - Sparged with 3 gal of water. With 90 min boil, want a lot of wort in the kettle. Last 1 gal of sparge might have been a little hot, around 170 - Started the propane burner to bring the heat back up simultaneously to save time. - Preboil gravity: 1.046

Boil: - To account for different hop styles and AAUs, hop schedule will be: 60 min - Mt. Hood 1 oz. @ 5.6 AAU (19 IBU), 30 min - Hallertau .5 oz @ 3.8% AAU (5 IBU), 1 min - Hallertau .5 oz @ 3.8% AAU (.25 IBU) - Added wort chiller at 10 min left instead of 20 - 90 min boil - Covered with foil then ran wort chiller for 20 min. - Transferred to carboy at 82 deg F and into fridge to come down in temp. Came down to 68 in 4-5 hours. Need to invest time in more ice bottles or another way to chill better.

Ferment: - Pitched yeast at 68 deg. (based on thermometer sticker on outside of plastic carboy) - OG: 1.050-1.052 - Overnight, temp was up to 72, no krausen yet but a little activity. Put in a cooler filled with water and one bottle filled with frozen water. Hoping to keep things cool without stressing the yeast. - After 1.5 days, krausen appeared. Ambient temp managed between 66-68 using cooler full of water and occasional frozen bottles. (Ferm temp may be a little higher). - After 7 days, krausen dropped and airlock slowed. Took a sample, gravity = 1.010-11. - Taste is good - has a distinct Belgian pepperiness (spicy esters), bready aroma and slight banana work really well together.

Age/Sour (began July 5th.): - Added 1oz of medium toast hungarian oak to boiling water. Cut heat, steeped for 10 min. Read a lot of different oaking instructions, so deferred to Mad Fermentationist blog. - Added dregs from 2 JP Oro de Calabaza Grand Reserve bottles. Learned that these are likely older bottles, so dregs were probably not as fresh as a regular bottle. - After 5 days, some large, yeasty bubbles on top of wort look like signs of souring. - 8/5 sample (1 month) - done while brewing another beer. Slight funk and tartness starting to come through but not a lot, still mostly getting Belgian yeast flavors (phenols?). SG: 1.008 - 10/1 sample (3 months) - smooth bitterness and spice, very subtle tartness. Could dry hop and time for either Thanksgiving or Xmas. - 11/1 sample (4 months) - SG: 1.008 (ready to bottle). Just a slight tang. Getting the Belgian yeast and a little brett funkiness but seems pretty balanced, smooth, and dry. No hops. Surprisingly, this might have been finished in as little as 1 month. Could use a little more tartness in next batch.

Dry hop: (began Nov 25th): 2 oz of Chinook (11.6%). Suspended in a hop bag with twine in same fermenter. Target 2 weeks.

Packaging: 12/9/17. OG: 1.008, added 4.6 oz sugar to 4.25 gallons of beer. Added .25 cup of fresh slurry from WLP550.
