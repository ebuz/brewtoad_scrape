Castor vs Pollux - Mosaic

an All Grain American IPA by eko

Brewed by eko

On May 20, 2016

Brewing Notes

Big Brew Day for the CCHB - what I requested for #40. Brewed in Akron. Beautiful day w/ just a couple small scattered showers. Late night, so a later start then we wanted, and a smaller crowd though about what was expected - brewers: Micah, Josh, and Rick.

Also my first 10 gallon batch. I brewed 10 gallons of the Castor vs Pollux - Mosaic so that I could leave 1/2 for Josh. K & I gave him a bucket and airlock for his birthday.

Propane was the biggest problem of the dat. One person brought one half-full (or so) tank, the other had two half-full (or so) tanks.

Notes

Date Note
05/20/2016 3:40pm

At 3:30 pm I messed around too much mashing in 7 gallons @ 172 F. (Next to this comment I wrote 168 in parenthesis - perhaps that means my strike water dropped 4 degrees. Not sure). In attempting 10 gallons the grain + water almost filled the entire mash tun. I hit 146-148 F and so added 3/4 gallon @ 192 F, stirred it in and the temp near the top was reading 154 F. Called it ready at 3:40 pm.
05/20/2016 4:40pm

Batch sparged: Sparged, added 4.5 gallons, sparged, added 4.5 gallons, sparged, added 1.5 gallons, sparged. I didn't track timings in detail as I usually do but didn't turn the heat on until 5:30 so that's a little more than 10 mins per sparge. Ended up with 14.5 gallons. This is in my 15 gallon pot.
05/20/2016 5:30pm

Turned the heat on under 14.5 gallons. The First Gravity reading showed 1.044. At 14.5 gallons x 44 this is 638 gravity points. I'm aiming for 11 gallons at the end, which would give me a gravity of 638 / 11 = 58 or 1.058.
05/20/2016 5:45pm

Switched propane tanks. At this point we tried to get strategic with what was used first and timing our tank refills. I'm sure we did the best we could but we sent Micah out w/ an empty one, when he returned he had the only one that was left and in half an hour we had to send him back out with another empty one so he had to go to a different & further away place.
05/20/2016 6:00pm

Boil reached in 30 minutes, that's 14.5 gallons and includes a propane tank swap after 15 minutes. Not bad. Added 3 oz Mosaic hops.
05/20/2016 6:45pm

Added 1 tsp Irish Moss.
05/20/2016 6:50pm

Added 2 oz Mosaic hops. 10 minutes left in boil.
05/20/2016 6:55pm

Added 2 oz Mosaic hops. 5 minutes left in boil. Began recirculating wort through the pump & CFC (but without using hose water) to help sanitize the equipment.
05/20/2016 7:00pm

Flame out. Added 2 oz Mosaic hops. Turned on the hose connected to the CFC to begin chilling.
05/20/2016 7:45pm

Finished distributing the wort between two buckets. Sanitized scissors and yeast, pitched the yeast. I took an Original Gravity reading at 1.046. I should've double checked that with a second sample. My starting gravity was 1.044 and I felt pretty comfortable with that, but a change to only 1.046 seems unlikely. I had just mixed up some StarSan and may have had some still in my pipette used to take the sample. Or maybe I took a sample from a bad spot. Who knows? I speculated at the time that I may have had 5.5 gallons in 2 buckets and 1 gallon in the lines. 1 gallon seems high, 1/2 gallon is more likely. But I didn't note whether I measured it or was just guessing. A note from bottling time says I have closer to 6 gallons (maybe 5.75), not the 5.5 gallons that I thought I had. OG was more likely 1.050 - 1.053.
06/23/2016 9:00pm

Using a swamp cooler, I kept the temperature at 68-70 F for ~2 weeks. Then I let it creep up to 73-74F for ~2 weeks. Tonight, after 33 days, I dry hopped 1.5 oz Mosaic directly into the fermentation bucket.
06/29/2016 8:00pm

Bottling day, finally, after 39 days in the fermentation bucket. Targeting 2.4 vols carbination for ~5.75 gallons @ 73 F I boiled 128 g sucrose in 1 cup water and bottled. I bottled 14 x 12oz and 22 x 22 oz (plus I took a gravity sample). My Final Gravity was 1.004.
