Notes

Date Note
07/30/2015 2:44am

According to http://www.brewersfriend.com/yeast-pitch-rate-and-starter-calculator/, brew requires 449 billion cells for 1.090 @ 5.5 gallons at a 1.0 cells/mL/plato rate. Planning 800mL starter (185 billion cells) stepped to 1.6L starter (415 billion cells).

Combined 800mL water with 80g Golden Light DME. Boiled in 1L flask 15 mins, cooled. Pitched WLP001 w/ exp. 10/16/15. Spun on stir plate.
07/31/2015 3:00am

Krausen fallen, vortex visible again in flask. Put flask in refrigerator.

08/01/2015 2:00am

Decanted most of the beer off of the 1L starter. Allowed to come up to room temperature over a few hours. Boiled 160g of Golden Light DME with 1600L (approx) of water in a 2L flask. Cooled to 76F. Pitched yeast from 1L flask into 2L flask. Spun on stir plate.
08/02/2015 4:00am

Put 2L yeast starter in fridge.
08/02/2015 5:00pm

Ran 5.16 gallons off of the tun. Measured 1.071 SG.

Used http://www.brewersfriend.com/dilution-and-boiloff-gravity-calculator/ to calculate that adding 2 gallons of water would bring our gravity down to 1.051.

Used the formula:
DMEWeight = ((Target SG x wort volume) - (Current SG x wort volume)) / 45
to determine how much DME would be required to bump gravity up to 1.067. Calculated 2.5 lbs - only have 2 lbs 3 oz on hand.

Added 2 lbs 3 oz Golden Light DME to kettle. Stirred. Measured 21.5% brix/1.090 SG. Added 2 gallons of water. Stirred. Measured post-extract to 15% brix, 1.061 SG.
08/02/2015 6:33pm

Calculated that 0.17oz Amarillo, 0.04oz Warrior, 0.04oz Simcoe would net roughly 94 IBU when added in 5 minute increments for 90 minutes. Ran out of Warrior after about 13 cups, sub'd Amarillo. Presently at 45 minutes into the boil.

08/02/2015 7:46pm

10 mins added Whirfloc. Hops were added to BIAB bag; pulled at flameout. Cooled to 81F. Aerated 2 minutes, transferred to carboy. Pitched from decanted 2L of WLP001.
08/04/2015 7:50pm

Calculated sugars necessary to increase wort/beer from OG 1.075 to "1.090".
Assumptions:
- Golden Light DME = 43 points per gallon (per Northern Brewer product description)
- Dextrose = 37 points per gallon (per http://merrycuss.com/calc/gravityadjustmentextract.html)

Points needed = (90 - 75) x 6 or 90 points.

Present "grain bill" up to 21.6875 lbs; should not use more than 5% dextrose according to various research. A max of 1 lbs should be used.

90 points - 37 points = 53 points
(will contribute 6.17 points to the overall gravity)

53 points / 43 points = 1.23 lbs. of DME required.
(will contribute 8.82 points to the overall gravity)

Will make a solution of 1.23 lbs DME and 1 lbs dextrose to get gravity up to "1.090".

---------------------------------------------------------

Decided to factor in the added water needed to dissolve the sugars. going to try for 1 qt.
Points needed = (90 - 75) x 6.25 or 93.75 points.
93.75 points - 37 points dextrose = 56.75 points remaining
56.75 points / 43 points = 1.32 lbs of DME required.

New solution is 1 qt water, 1 lbs dextrose, 1.32 lbs (1 lb 5 oz) Golden light DME.

08/05/2015 3:21am

Combined 1 lb dextrose with 21.10 oz of Golden Light DME and 1 qt of water. Made pancake batter. Heated to boil. Cooled. Made delicious malt caramel. Ate some, poured the rest down the drain :)
08/25/2015 3:34am

Measured 9.8% brix, 1.017 FG. Racked to secondary, added 3 oz whole leaf cascade

09/07/2015 10:36pm

Per Homebrew Dad (http://www.brewunited.com/priming_sugar_calculator.php) - 103g of dextrose for 4.5 gallons of beer at 65F for an American IPA. Boiled w/ 2 cups of tap water for 5 minutes. Cooled.

Pulled hop sack. Transferred to bottling bucket. Sample reads 9.7% brix, 1.016 FG. Tasty as hell!
