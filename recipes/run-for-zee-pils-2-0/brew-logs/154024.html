Brewing Notes

PILS - 5/2

5/1 Had to make a 2 step starter (see screenshot attached). Adjusted the amount of gypsum and calcium chloride (by about 10-20%).

5/2 6:20 AM heating HLT Yeast starter has been going for 11 hrs 8:24 AM, vorlouf, laughter 2.1 gal (SG, 1.086 using refractometer) 8:45 AM, strike with 168 degrees, site for 5 min

Notes: 1.037 pilsen malt 37*10 = 370 maximum potential of pilsner grains

if I have 5.5 Gal x 1.050 = 275GU Need 1.036 SG... 7.6 Gal @ 1.036 = 273.6

273.6 (actual GU) / 370 (potential GU) = 73.9% Efficiency

FOR BOIL (90 min boil) Need to boil down to 5.58 to get 1.049 OG 9:15am - hot break, very good boil, no wind outside, optimal environment.

Lost 1 tank of propane after 25 min, switched to second one, may run out of that tooâ€¦Lost second tank with 18 min left, moved to inside boiler, after a few min got back to rolling boil

10:50am, flameout, warm outside, so put hose in ice water to chill the incoming water

FERM 9:45am - too yeast off stirplate, moved to fermenter to acclimate to lower temp. Will add fermenter and let that cool to around same temp & pitch.

Refractometer reading - 1.050, 12.8 BRIX

5.75 gal post boil, 1.047 on refractometer, 65 degrees 5.5 gal in fermenter, 1.049 on refractometer (12.2 BRIX), 1.051 on hydrometer

5.75 * 49 = 289.75 GU

Letting yeast / wort come to same temp Will oxygenate wort & add yeast

12:50pm: Oxygenated for 30 seconds, added 2L, 2 step starter. I'd made 1.5L on 5/1, but that + the first step of yeast put it to 2L

5/20 - Diacetyl rest begins, raise to 66. Sat at 65-66 for a full 24 hrs 5/23 - began cold crash. Set temp control down to 32. Ideally will Keg Thursday afternoon, 5/24 5/28 - wasn't able to keg on 5/24, beer sat at 31/32 from 5/24 until 5/28 in the afternoon. Kegged 5gal., put small amount of PSI on beer.
