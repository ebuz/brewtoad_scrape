Mai im Sumpf

an All Grain Maibock/Helles Bock by jll23

Brewed by jll23

On May 22, 2017

Brewing Notes

Mashing with a little over 2 gallons of water. That volume is comprised of a little over .5 gallon of distilled water, with the rest made up of campden treated tap water.

Will sparge with a little under 1.5 gallons of treated tap water.

Vienna and Munich are Avangard this time. CaraHell is obviously Weyermann.

Wort is looking pretty dark, even before the boil.

Added a couple of pellets towards the end of the boil, just for a hint of hop flavor.

Cooled wort down to about 67F, before putting it in the carboy.

Wort tastes malty, but still pretty light. No real intense flavors. Some pleasant/mild underlying bitterness.

Added almost all of the yeast, after re-hydrating it in room temp water for about 30 minutes. Interested to see how much krausen this is going to make. Hopefully not enough that I need the tube.

Going towards the higher end with priming sugar this time. This is a malty beer and good carbonation will balance that. Also, the beer is in the low 70's, which is a little warmer than the beers I've recently bottled (a trend that will no doubt continue throughout summer).

Cat got too curious right during transfer to the bottling bucket. I had to shoo her away, but in the process I temporarily lost siphon suction. In order to finish, I ended up having to yeast the beer a lot more than I would have liked. Hopefully it'll just settle out again with time.

Taste

At bottling, this this beer seems pretty great. Definitely looks like an Oktoberfest and the malt flavor reflects that as well. Lots of intense bready malts with some spice. It tastes sweet (and for good reason), but there's some really smooth bitterness that creeps up at the end. Minimal yeast flavor at this point. A little interesting fruitiness from the hops, but not much. These malt combinations are the closest I've ever gotten to an Oktoberfest flavor. Maybe more of a 2x Oktoberfest flavor. Hope I don't mess it up in the bottle.

It's been about a month in the bottle. I've been drinking these on and off basically since I put them in there. I've liked it a lot for the whole duration. It's obviously a malty beer. Reddish oktoberfest hue. Not too sweet, not bitter. Can't taste the hops much anymore. Very bready. I echo the initial impression that it's got almost a dry brown ale flavor, just with a distinctive German flair. If I was really going for a maibock, I would mix in some pils for vienna, but I do really like this beer as it is. The yeast is fairly "neutral'' but upon closer examination this time, it does have a little Germanic funk to it. Subtle, and goes well with all the malt. Solid counter balance. Head-retention could be better, but most of them have been okay. I've never used cara-hell before, and as the malt flavor in this one seem kind of unique to me, I do wonder if that's what I'm tasting. There are some Munich notes, but they're pretty subtle. Fair amount of smooth spice from the Vienna. Very malty beer, a little yeast funk, hops are just enough to blend in keep it on the balanced to slightly-bitter side of the spectrum. Good stuff.

Almost 2 months in the bottle. I have enjoyed this beer immensely. It is a very deep orange/red beer. I really just want to be able to follow this up with another based on what I've learned from it, but it'll probably be a few months before the conditions are right again. Weirdly, as time has gone on, the funky/sulfuric aspects from the yeast have increased. I don't mind them at all, this is part of the understated glory of German ale-ery. This beer is a little spicy yet, but more so extremely malty in the darker ranges of that flavor. With all that, the balance is definitely towards the sweet and malty. I am somewhat inspired to try and brew a beer that is similarly made up of a lot of vienna with a bit more bitterness. More in the German IPA realm. Regardless, this is a nice beer. The yeast is distinctive in it's funkiness. I could go either way in recreating this with the same yeast, or using a Kolsch yeast or even the far less funky White Labs German ale yeast. If one wanted it less malty (and it is very malty, if delicious), it would be easy enough to swap some pils in for some of the Vienna.

Here we are, nearly 5 months after brewing. Still amazingly malty, tasty, spicy, a little (German) funky. I'm not sure why I was reminded of this descriptor, but "dank" comes to mind. Not in terms of hops. I have never prescribed to that line of thinking. More like the beer of the same name from a certain Wisconsin brewery. Think reddish malt personified in taste. This is a German version of that idea, I guess. The beer tastes like it has since it's creation. Supremely malty. Intense, pushing the envelope, but not sweet. It's a dry, spicy malt. Echoes of those sort of "imperial oktoberfest" beers that have sprung up in the states. The funk, that dry, woody, slightly sulfuric aroma, really cuts through the sweet and elevates it to something more. German ales. Versatile and amazingly complex.

Killing off what I think is the last bottle of this after 5.5 months in the bottle. Everything I said previously still sticks, but I was struck this time by how fruity it seems, especially in the beginning. Some of this is the sweet malt, but some of it is from yeast esters. Really nice combo, in conjunction with all the other elements (the spice, the dank malt, all that). Tastes remarkably good and fresh after this long, too. I really like some of the other widely available German ale yeasts, but this one, with the fruit and the funk, has a cool niche as well.

Notes

Date Note
05/23/2017 2:00pm

Fermentation began, visibly, last night. So far, the temperature is hanging around 70F. That's a bit higher than I want it, so I'm trying to gradually cool things down. Luckily, the next few days call for 50's and 60's. Hopefully I can basically get it to complete primary before the weather heats up again. I did pitch a lot of yeast, so that should make it go quick (as well as work against ester production--I would like this beer fairly clean).

05/23/2017 7:35pm

I've been struggling to get this thing a little cooler for most of the day. It still is around 70F.

I've got to say, despite the information about this yeast consisting almost exclusively of talk about its impressive head while fermenting, it doesn't seem very noteworthy to me. At least so far, it looks pretty much like any other German ale yeast, and even then there are some that produce way larger karausens than this. Right now the krausen is actually green with hop matter, so it doubly doesn't look like advertised.

05/23/2017 11:28pm

Alright, so the krausen has really inflated over the last few hours. If it keeps expanding much longer, the tube will be in order.

05/24/2017 12:40am

Fine, I stand corrected. Just as top-cropping as some other German strains. Tube is in place.
05/24/2017 4:10pm

Temperature has dropped to about 68F. Fermentation has had a corresponding drop in ferocity. Still trucking away, though.

06/06/2017 4:23pm

In the second week of fermentation, temperatures rose all the way into the higher 70's. When it finally cooled off again (last night), the crash back to the lower 70's took most of the persistent krausen with it. I'll now probably give it a day or two to settle again (after being moved) before bottling. It's not that large of a beer, a lot of yeast was pitched, and said yeast swarm worked in elevated temperatures towards the end of what would be a normal primary fermentation period.
