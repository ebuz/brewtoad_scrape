Mango IPA

an All Grain American IPA by Ben Ireland

  • 1 Brew Logs
  • 0 Variants

Export BeerXML

Brewed by Ben Ireland

On July 31, 2013

Brewing Notes

8/8

IPA - Rack 1 gallon to secondary, dry hop w/ .5 oz Citra pellets. G = 1.019

Mango - Add 9lb Fresh Mango, pureed to secondary. Rack remaining 3.75 gallon onto fruit. - According to calculations, G = 1.031

Fruit: 2nd Fermentation slowed down on 8/11 and no action in airlock after 8/12 Dry Hop: No action in air lock after 8/10

8/15

Rack IPA into 3rd, for clarity. G = 1.019

Rack Mango IPA into 3rd for clarity. G = 1.015

8/19

Boil 2/3 C corn sugar in 2 C water = 16 oz of priming solution

Rack IPA to bottling jug w/ 3.5 oz of priming suolution. FG = 1.019

Rack Mango IPA to bottling bocket w/ 12.5 oz of priming solution. FG = 1.013

Rack to bottles and sit for 7-10 days

8/25

Cracked a bottle of each. Nailed it!

Taste

IPA - Poured from bottle to tulip glass. Pours dark brown, with huge tan head, great retention. Smells of citrus, mango, pine, sweetness, malts show through. Tastes of citrus and malt. Very smooth mouth feel, porter-like, with a great lingering of Citra hops. Nice lacing. A somewhat heavy beer, but easy to drink at only 5.2%. Not an IPA, I will call it a Dark Ale. "Come Heavy or Don't Come at Ale"

Mango - Pours a beautiful light brown opaque (unfiltered) Almost resembles an Abbey ale. Thin white head, fades quickly. Smells of super sweet mango way up front, with a hit of citrus and malt. Mango comes first in taste followed by the malts and Citrus from the hops. Very easy drinker. Also, not an IPA, I will call it Amika's Mango Ale. 6.2%
