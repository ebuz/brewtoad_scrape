Brewed by Mark Stone

On March 12, 2014

Brewing Notes

Strike water = 166, mash settled at ~148. Wanted to raise temp to 151, ended up at 153 and it stayed pretty stable at this temp.

LME was Black Rock LIGHT from NZ. (It's a Pilsen malt-based extract.) Water was Labrador bottled water.

Taste

Taste Rating
40 / 50

Pours a hazy amber/red with a half-finger dense white head. (Under-primed it.)

Aroma is dominated by hops, though the presence is faint. Alcohol is present but not overwhelming.

Taste is malt forward with strong caramel notes. While the overall bitterness level is immense, it seems round and soft in character. The Citra hops come across as fruity and compliment the Munich and crystal malts well. Alcohol taste is almost undetectable despite the 8.6% ABV.

Mouth-feel is thick, almost syrupy. Wish it was drier.

Lacing is beautiful. Beer leaves an intricate lace from the top all the way down. Had this beer been carbonated correctly, the head retention would have been good.

Note for future brewing: Definitely needs way more dry hops. There is very little hops on the nose.
