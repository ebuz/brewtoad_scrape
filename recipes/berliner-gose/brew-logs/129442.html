Berliner + Gose

an All Grain Specialty Beer by stuartgrant

  • Ingredients
  • 1 Brew Logs
  • 0 Variants

Print
Export BeerXML

Brewed by stuartgrant

On December 15, 2014

Brewing Notes

Split batch: will yeild 20L Berliner at 1.030 OG, plus 10L Gose at 1.045 OG. Only the Gose will be boiled; the Berliner will be no boil, and diluted from 1.035 down to 1.030. The Gose will take slightly more of the first runnings to yeild pre boil SG of around 1.040 which, after a 60min boil, should be around 1.045.

Both will be cooled to around 30°C and pitched with WLP672 Lactobacillus Brevis. Both will get WLP644 Brett Trois added after about 24hours. I will use heat belt to keep the Berliner warm, but the Gose will just be ambient.

Fermentation Checks

Date Temperature Gravity Notes
12/14/2014 11:00pm 28.0 degC 1.030

[BERLINER DAY 0] Pitched 1L starter of active Lactobacillus Brevis and attached heat belt. No aeration. Added 7.5g lactic acid (88%) to bring wort pH down to 4.39. This is supposed to prevent an enzyme produced by Lacto from degrading proteins required for head retention. Fermentation took off within a few hours.
12/20/2014 5:28am 20.0 degC 1.013

[BERLINER DAY 5] pH is now 3.89 (good, but I still fear that this won't drop much more). SG is much higher than I thought it would be, but hopefully this means a long slow finish which will increase funk and acidity.
01/07/2015 9:37am 22.0 degC 1.009

[GOSE DAY 24] Like the Berliner, this is not very sour - pH is 4.02. Tastes pretty good actually. But definitely need to sour it.
12/15/2014 8:00pm 22.0 degC 1.022

[BERLINER DAY 1] Activity slowed down markedly this morning; not sure if just temp related or if the Lacto is genuinely slowing down. pH down to 4.01 which is okay, but I fear that it won't go significantly lower now that the temp is down. Added 1000mL starter of Brett Trois. Leaving at ambient temps, but if it drops below 22°C during the day I will apply heat belt tonight.
12/15/2014 2:00am 33.0 degC 1.045

[GOSE DAY 0] Pitched 500mL starter of Lactobacillus Brevis, a bit warmer than the Berliner, but without heat belt. Also added 4.5g lactic acid (88%), although I didn't measure pH. Once this cools down to 25°C or below I will pitch the Brett. The wort tasted quite interesting - not salty at all, but you could just detect it, and it will probably be easier to taste in the finished beer. I can always add more at bottling. The coriander seed was also quite subtle, but definitely there.
{+18 HOURS] Dropped to 22°C. Pitched around 500mL starter of Brett Trois. Didn't check pH or gravity.
01/13/2015 6:41am 21.0 degC —

[BERLINER DAY 30] Rather than adding other sour bugs, I decided to add 2kg organic blueberries (picked and frozen yesterday), to see if that would get the Brevis going. The fruit was still frozen, so the temp quickly dropped to 14°C, but I applied heat belt and am aiming to get up around 25-30°C to favour the Lactobacillus. It's an experiment, but we'll see how it goes!
01/15/2015 9:42pm 31.0 degC 1.006

[BERLINER DAY 33] Very active fermentation; blueberries are losing their colour, and the wort is now bright pink. Wort pH is down to 3.69, which is good! Feeding the Lacto with fruit seems to have worked - I doubt the acidity from the blueberries would have dropped the pH that much alone. Tastes very strongly of Lacto - a slightly milky flavour, even - so it will need time to clean up. I will probably pitch more Brett once it slows down and I drop the temp back to 20-25°C in a few days.
01/07/2015 9:34am 22.0 degC 1.006

[BERLINER DAY 24] That damn Brevis; pH is now 3.98 (higher). Taste is clean but insipid. Need to find a way to get this sour - either waiting or pitching other bugs (or blending) seem like my options. Annoying, when I was aiming for a quick sour.
02/04/2015 10:24pm 18.0 degC 1.004

[BERLINER DAY 52] Ready to keg now. Forgot to check pH, but it's no more sour in flavour, which makes it a little insipid. I think I'll add some lactic acid to the keg to increase acidity. Blueberry flavour is subtle but pleasant. Should be a really nice beer once it's done.
03/22/2015 1:16am 18.0 degC 1.007

[GOSE DAY 97] Dropped another 2 points SG (to 5.0% abv), but has still not soured at all - pH has actually risen to 4.07, although that could be because I recalibrated it since last check. Flavour is really clean and pleasant - just the right amount of coriander (maybe a little more salt would be good), along with a nice apple aromatic. I decided to blend this with an Apricot Sour Blonde that I brewed which is at an oppressive 3.29 pH. The resultant 21L of beer came out at 3.5 pH (which is ideal, although it might drop more), and I'll be leaving it for a month or so before bottling.
03/22/2015 1:11am 18.0 degC 1.003

[BERLINER DAY 97] After 2 months on the blueberries, and 5 weeks since adding lactic acid, I kegged this beer. The flavour is fantastic! Really clean sourness - not too sharp, and none of the medicinal flavour others have experienced from adding lactic acid. Final pH is 3.38 which seems to be perfect. The blueberry flavour is there, and really enjoyable, but you wouldn't necessarily pick it as blueberry - especially from the reddish pink colour. This is exactly what I wanted from this beer, even if it took far longer than I had hoped to get it there.
01/18/2015 8:49pm 18.0 degC 1.004

[BERLINER DAY 35] Turned off hear belt after 4 days - temp had been 32°C on the hottest day and down to 27°C when I turned it off. pH now 3.67, so barely changed from 3 days ago. Taste is lightly tart, and will be enjoyable, but not what I wanted. I suspect most of the pH drop is from the fruit acidity. Blueberries have mostly dropped to the bottom now, so I think I'll wait a little longer then rack into a keg.
02/15/2015 1:02am 20.0 degC 1.003

[BERLINER DAY 62] One month with blueberries. Surprisingly it's dropped another point in SG, but the pH was the same at 3.67. I pulled 200mL and dosed 40mL samples with 88% lactic acid to see what final pH tasted best. In the end, I decided on 50mL (41grams) lactic acid per 20L, which should lower the pH to around 3.4. I added this right into secondary, and plan to keg/bottle in the next week.
