Brewing Notes

Fermentation Checks

Date Temperature Gravity Notes
07/09/2014 12:17pm 4.0 degC 1.011

[72 days] bottled 19L with 900mL reserved wort. Racked to secondary with dry hops 9 days ago, and put into fridge 2 days ago to clear. Will need at least 4-6 weeks to carbonate I think. Tastes pretty incredible - huge aroma from dry hops, flavour melds with pineapple esters and a slight tartness, bitter finish which hopefully will mellow with age and carbonation.
04/28/2014 10:31am 22.0 degC 1.054

Racked into fermenter with some splashing, but didn't use aeration kit. Pitched about 150mL slurry from a ~4 litre batch of Brett beer that had been dormant for about 4 weeks. Suspect this is a serious under-pitch for 23 litres, but can't find figures on how to estimate cell count for Brettanomyces. [EDIT: took off rather quickly, so must have been almost enough cells!]
06/27/2014 12:05am 15.0 degC 1.011

[+60 days] Dropped very clear and no airlock activity. I reckon this is done, so planning on dry-hopping it soon and bottling in a week. Tastes super clean - just a hint of earthy phenolics, but could easily be mistaken for a Belgian yeast. Great floral notes and pineapple esters. Not sour at all, and definitely not acetic detectable, but refreshing acidity.
05/15/2014 11:37am 16.0 degC 1.024

[+17 days] Not too bad given that I didn't pitch *that* much yeast, and that it's been around 15-16째C which is well below recommended temps for Brett.

Flavour is really intriguing! Very fruity, with a pineapple/kiwi kind of acidity, hint of acetic acid too, and then a bit of funk in the aftertaste - more like musty/corky than the less flattering but more traditional Brett descriptors (sweaty horse blanket ...?).

No longer smells like farts.

Date Note
04/28/2014 6:36am

Speedy afternoon brew. Mash was 35min at 64C with gas on LOW. Mash rose to 66C after 25mins - gas OFF. Mash dropped to 65째C after 35min. Added 6L boiling water and hit 71C. Held for 10mins. No mash out; vorlauf and batch sparge. Added 9L water at 82C for second batch sparge. Collected 26.5L at 1.051 SG. 72% efficiency = pretty good for a 60min mash/sparge.
