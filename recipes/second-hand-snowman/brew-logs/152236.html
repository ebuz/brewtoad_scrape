Second Hand Snowman

an All Grain Special/Best/Premium Bitter by Rollermt

  • 1 Brew Logs
  • 0 Variants

Print
Export BeerXML

Brewed by Rollermt

On December 03, 2017

Brewing Notes

Prep: - Preboiled water - Yeast starter - Store didn't have Northern Brewer hops, so replaced them with Challenger. (Challenger is used in Fuller's ESB). - Selected Golden Promise malt instead of other UK options.

Mash: - Prepared a step mash per BCS book's recommendation for a witbier. https://www.brewersfriend.com/mash/ - Step 1: Target: Mash 3 gal water @ 122 (Strike target: 130), 20 min. Actual: Strike 131, Mash 124. (Expecting temp to drop a few degrees.) - Step 2: Target: Mash 5 gal @ 154 (Strike target: boil), 40 min. Actual: Mash 156. Dropped to 145 over 40 minute period (9 degree drop). - Boil went as planned. Wort chiller and whirlfloc at 20 min. - Orange and allspice went in after boil. 0 min. - OG: 1.040, lower than anticipated. - Only collected ~4 gallons of wort. Planning to add additional sugar and carrots to make up difference and increase gravity. - Orange and allspice aroma may consider adding more in a few days as a "dry hop" element. - Simmered 1# of sugar (half white, half brown) for 15 min. to add to wort. - Roasted ~8 carrots at 325 for 1 hour (lightly browned). Added to some boiling water to create 24 oz of puree, approx. 2 lbs. Put in fridge to chill for 1-2 days.

Ferment: - Pitched yeast cool, at 57 degrees. (Wort stayed outside for awhile.) - Overnight, activity is present. At 24 hours, plenty of activity and krausen. Fermentation temp is 68 (fermometer), so potentially even higher. I'm okay with this higher temp because I want some of the esters and I want it to finish fast. - 12/5/17. Fermentation was active but had slowed slightly. SG: 1.020 (a little over halfway done). Added carrot puree and .5 tsp of allspice since there wasn't a lot of spice aroma. Yeast aroma was very reminiscent of a wit, fruity and spicy probably from the warm ferm temp, orange peel, and spices. - 12/7/17. Temp had dropped from 68 to 64 and fermentation was slowing, so brought carboy up to first floor to warm up. Temp back up to 68. Hoping to finish slightly drier this way. - 12/10/17: SG: 1.006. Beer is probably finished and can be packaged. Aroma was very wit-like. Taste has some unique characters. Allspice gave it a unique angle, but I almost wish I was smelling coriander since it was close. Carrot may be contributing something earthy there too. Slight bitterness on the back of the palate I thought was too strong at first, but then it seemed to go away. Lara didn't notice this bitterness. (Overall, wondering if maybe it needs a little more maltiness to balance the bitterness?)
